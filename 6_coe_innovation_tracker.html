Innovation
Tracker

HR innovation at BaKuu Holding is not about chasing trends. It is about running small, deliberate experiments that make the HR function measurably better over time — and knowing which experiments to scale, which to close, and which to learn from regardless of outcome.

The Innovation Discipline at BaKuu Holding
A one-person CoE cannot innovate on everything at once. The tracker enforces a discipline: no more than three active experiments at any time, each with a clear hypothesis, a defined test period, and a measurable success criterion.
HR innovation fails when it is too ambitious, too vague, or never closed. A CHRO-level CoE does not run pilots that last forever. It runs 8-week experiments with binary outcomes: adopt or close. The innovation tracker makes that discipline visible, forces decisions, and builds an institutional record of what BaKuu Holding has tried, what worked, and what did not. That record is itself a competitive asset.

Section 02 — Innovation Philosophy
Innovation
Philosophy

Three principles govern how BaKuu Holding approaches HR innovation. They are not aspirational — they are operational rules that determine how the CoE spends its limited experimental capacity.

Why Most HR Innovation Fails
HR innovation fails in three predictable ways: it is too big to test quickly, it has no measurable success criterion, or it is never formally closed when it is not working.
The BaKuu Holding innovation philosophy is designed to prevent all three failure modes. Small bets means the experiment can be completed in 8–12 weeks. Binary outcomes means the CoE commits upfront to what success looks like and closes the experiment if the criterion is not met. Learning always means a closed experiment is data, not a failure.
Principle 01Small Bets, Fast Cycles
Every experiment at BaKuu Holding is designed to be completable in 8–12 weeks with a single HRBP and a defined cohort. If an idea cannot be tested in that timeframe, it is redesigned into a smaller test or broken into a sequence of smaller experiments.
Applied at BaKuu Holding
The G4 coaching quality experiment ran for 8 weeks in Thailand with the Q3 2025 G4 cohort (4 coaches, 11 direct reports). It did not require buy-in from all four countries before starting. Thailand proved the concept; the data justified the group-wide rollout.
Principle 02Binary Outcomes, Always
Before any experiment begins, the CoE defines a binary success criterion: a specific, measurable outcome that will determine whether the experiment is adopted or closed. Saying we will learn something useful is not a success criterion.
Applied at BaKuu Holding
The structured onboarding check-in experiment defined its criterion upfront: 30-day retention of key HR process knowledge assessed by HRBP quiz, target score 70% or above. When the pilot cohort scored 74%, the experiment was adopted. When a peer onboarding buddy experiment scored 51%, it was closed.
Principle 03Learning Always, Regardless of Outcome
A closed experiment is not a failure — it is data. The CoE captures the hypothesis, what was tested, what was measured, what the outcome was, and what it implies for future experiments. That record is filed in the KM system and reviewed at the quarterly check-in.
Applied at BaKuu Holding
The informal manager feedback loop experiment was closed in Q4 2025 after 10 weeks. The outcome did not meet the success criterion. But the learning — that Myanmar SMs required a structured prompt format rather than open-ended requests — directly informed the SM Feedback Prompt Library idea now top of the backlog.
Section 03 — Idea Capture
Idea
Capture

Ideas enter the innovation tracker from five sources. Every idea is captured using the same template: enough structure to assess it at the quarterly review, not so much that the friction kills the idea before it is logged.

The Capture Discipline
An idea that is not written down does not exist. A HRBP who notices that Indonesia SMs consistently misapply the PIP process has identified an innovation opportunity — but only if it is captured before the next urgent task replaces it.
The CoE maintains a living idea backlog. Anyone in the HR function can add to it using the capture template. The backlog is reviewed quarterly at the CoE innovation check-in. Ideas not progressed within two quarters are either escalated to the prioritisation queue or closed with a brief rationale.
Five Idea Sources
HRBP Field Observation
A HRBP notices a recurring pattern — a process that consistently breaks down, a tool that is consistently misused, or a gap that is consistently filled informally.
Example: Indonesia HRBP notices G3 hires consistently underperform in first 60 days. Structured onboarding deficit identified.
CoE Benchmark Review
The CoE conducts a biennial benchmark of HR practice at comparable SE Asia companies. Gaps between BaKuu Holding practice and benchmark generate ideas.
Example: Benchmark finds peer companies use structured stay interviews for high-potential G4s. BaKuu Holding does not.
People Analytics Signal
The People Analytics dashboard flags an anomaly — a metric moving in an unexpected direction suggesting a process or design problem.
Example: Myanmar attrition rate increases 6pp in two quarters. Hypothesis: exit interview data is not being used to inform retention action.
Country SM Feedback
A Country SM raises a concern or suggestion via their HRBP about how an HR process is working or not working in their country context.
Example: Laos SM raises that the annual performance review cycle does not align with the field operations calendar.
Post-Experiment Learning
A closed experiment identifies a secondary hypothesis worth testing — something the experiment revealed that was not the original focus.
Example: Closed informal manager feedback experiment reveals structured prompts increase SM response rates. New idea: test a structured prompt library.
Idea Capture Template
The Two-Minute RuleIf the idea cannot be captured in two minutes, it is too complex to be an idea at this stage. Break it down first.
The Problem or Opportunity
In one sentence: what is not working, what is missing, or what could be measurably better?
The Source
HRBP observation / CoE benchmark / analytics signal / SM feedback / post-experiment learning.
The Hypothesis
If we do X, then Y will improve by Z. One sentence. Falsifiable.
The Affected Population
Which country, grade, role, or cohort? How many people?
The Rough Effort
Low (1–2 weeks CoE time) / Medium (3–5 weeks) / High (6+ weeks). A gut estimate at capture stage.
Captured By and Date
Who logged this and when. Ideas are not owned at capture stage.

Section 04 — Prioritisation
Prioritisation
Model

Not every idea becomes an experiment. The CoE reviews the backlog quarterly and scores each idea against four criteria. The top-scoring ideas that fit within the 3-experiment cap are promoted to Testing. The rest remain in the backlog or are closed with a rationale.

The Prioritisation Discipline
Prioritisation is not about finding the best idea. It is about finding the best idea that can be tested right now, given current CoE capacity, country readiness, and strategic priorities.
The CoE runs three experiments at any one time. No more. Every quarter the CoE asks: which three ideas, if tested and adopted, would make the biggest measurable difference to BaKuu Holding HR performance in the next 12 months?
Scoring Model — Four Criteria
Each idea is scored 1–5 on four criteria. Maximum score: 20. Ideas scoring 14+ are strong candidates for the next experiment slot.

Prioritisation Rules & Process
The 3-Experiment CapThe CoE runs a maximum of 3 active experiments simultaneously. A new experiment cannot start until an existing one reaches Adopted or Closed.
01
Quarterly Backlog Review
The CoE reviews all backlog ideas at the quarterly innovation check-in. Each idea is scored against the four criteria. Top-scoring ideas within cap are nominated for the next experiment slot.
02
HRBP Input on Feasibility
Before any idea is promoted from backlog to Testing, the HRBP for the proposed pilot country is consulted. If the country is not operationally ready, the idea is deferred one quarter.
03
Hypothesis Sharpening
Ideas promoted from backlog must have their hypothesis sharpened into a testable, falsifiable statement with a specific success criterion before the experiment is designed.
04
Experiment Design Before Start
No experiment starts without a one-page experiment design: hypothesis, test design, cohort, duration, success criterion, data collection method, and responsibility allocation.
05
Ideas Aged Over 2 Quarters
Any idea that has sat in the backlog for more than two quarters without being promoted is either escalated as a deliberate choice, or closed with a rationale filed in the KM system.

Section 05 — Active Experiments
Active
Experiments

Three experiments are currently running at BaKuu Holding. Each has a defined hypothesis, a clear test design, a binary success criterion, and a target close date. The CoE reviews each experiment monthly and formally assesses outcomes at close.

EXP-13
G4 Coaching Quality Scorecard
Stage · Country · Close
Testing · All Countries
Started Feb 2026 · Closes May 2026
Hypothesis
If G4 coaches are assessed against a structured coaching quality scorecard each quarter, the G4-to-G5 conversion rate will increase from 62% to 68% within 12 months.
Test Design
The CoE developed a 6-dimension coaching quality rubric. HRBPs score each G4 coach quarterly across: clarity of expectations, feedback frequency, development conversation quality, challenge quality, recognition practice, and succession awareness. Scores are discussed at the monthly HRBP–SM check-in.
Cohort
All G4 coaches across 4 countries (n=11). Baseline scores captured Feb 2026. First quarterly assessment May 2026.
Success Criterion (Binary)
Average coaching quality score of 70+ (out of 100) across all countries by Q3 2026, AND G4 conversion rate improves by at least 4pp within 12 months of adoption.
Lead
CoE (design and calibration) + Country HRBPs (quarterly scoring)
Current Status
Baseline captured. Thailand 74, Indonesia 61, Myanmar 58, Laos 66. Significant variance confirms the rubric is discriminating. Indonesia and Myanmar HRBPs scheduled for additional CoE support before first quarterly assessment.

Structured Exit Interview Activation
Stage · Country · Close
Testing · Myanmar
Started Jan 2026 · Closes Apr 2026
Hypothesis
If exit interview data is reviewed by the Myanmar HRBP monthly and shared with the SM, specific retention actions will be implemented within 30 days of signal, reducing voluntary attrition by 5pp within 6 months.
Test Design
Myanmar HRBP conducts a structured exit interview for every departure using a CoE-designed template. Monthly theme synthesis is shared with the SM in a 30-minute session. SM commits to one retention action per month based on the data.
Cohort
All Myanmar voluntary departures Jan–Apr 2026 (estimated 4–6 exits). SM retention action log maintained monthly.
Success Criterion (Binary)
At least one retention action implemented per month for 3 consecutive months, AND voluntary attrition rate in Myanmar reduces by 5pp or more within 6 months of full adoption.
Lead
Myanmar HRBP (interviews and synthesis) + Myanmar SM (action commitment) + CoE (template and review)
Current Status
Month 1 (Jan): 2 exit interviews completed. Theme: limited career pathway visibility for G2 field roles. SM committed to monthly career conversations for all G2s. Month 2 (Feb): 1 exit interview. Theme consistent. SM action implemented. On track.

Section 07 — Learning & Scaling
Learning &
Scaling

The moment an experiment ends — adopted or closed — a learning record is created. That record is filed in the KM system, reviewed at the quarterly innovation check-in, and used to inform future experiment design.

The Compounding Effect
Six adopted experiments since 2024. Each one made something measurably better. Together, they compound: the coaching frequency standard improved G4 development conversations, the calibration standard made those conversations comparable across countries, and the coaching quality scorecard now running will make them consistently excellent.
This is what a one-person CoE looks like at 24 months in: not a single grand transformation, but a sequence of small, well-designed experiments that each build on the last. The learning register is the evidence of that compounding and the argument for the CHRO positioning in 2027. A CoE that can show 13 experiments, 6 adoptions, and a traceable line from each adoption to a measurable business outcome has earned the right to lead at the executive level.
Learning Record — What Gets Captured
Every experiment generates a learning record within 5 working days of close. One page maximum. Filed always, regardless of outcome.
The Hypothesis (as stated at start)
Reproduced exactly as written when the experiment was designed. Not retrospectively modified.
What Was Tested
The actual test design as implemented. Note any deviations from the planned design and why they occurred.
The Outcome
Did the experiment meet its success criterion? State the actual measured result versus the criterion. Binary: met or not met.
What This Implies
If adopted: what does this tell us about what works at BaKuu Holding? If closed: what does this tell us about what does not work, and why?
What to Test Next
One or two follow-on hypotheses generated by this experiment. These go directly into the idea backlog with a reference to this learning record.
Filed In KM
The learning record is filed in the Innovation domain of the KM system, cross-referenced to the framework or process it relates to.
Scaling Protocol — Pilot to Group-Wide
The Scaling RuleAn experiment is never scaled group-wide immediately. It moves from pilot country to a second country for validation, then group-wide. This prevents a Thailand success that does not transfer to Myanmar from becoming a group-wide failure.
01
Pilot Outcome Assessment
Experiment meets success criterion in pilot country. Learning record created. CoE assesses whether the result is likely to transfer to other country contexts.
02
Validation Country
CoE selects a second country for validation, typically the country most different from the pilot in operating context. Validation runs 4–6 weeks.
03
Framework Integration
If validation confirms the result, the CoE integrates the innovation into the relevant HR framework or creates a new one, following the Framework Lifecycle creation process (Tool 5).
04
Group-Wide Communication
CoE communicates the adoption to all HRBPs: what changed, why it changed, what they need to do differently, and what support is available.
05
Post-Adoption Monitoring
At the first scheduled framework review after adoption, the CoE assesses whether the innovation is delivering the expected outcome at group-wide scale.
Key Learnings — From Closed Experiments
EXP-09 — Peer Onboarding Buddy AssignmentClosed
Why it closedBuddy knowledge transfer score 51% against a 70% criterion. The experiment assumed buddies would know how to transfer knowledge effectively without guidance. They did not — they socialised rather than structured.
Learning applied in
Informed the design of the 30-Day HRBP Onboarding Knowledge Quiz (EXP-12), which replaced unstructured buddy knowledge transfer with a structured CoE-led quiz and reference pack. EXP-12 was subsequently adopted.
EXP-06 — Informal Manager Feedback LoopClosed
Why it closedSM feedback quality score did not meet the criterion after 10 weeks. Myanmar SMs treated the open-ended prompt as optional rather than required, and when they did respond, the feedback was too general to be actionable.
Learning applied in
Directly informed the SM Feedback Prompt Library idea (B-02 in backlog, currently top-scored). The learning: structure is not optional for Myanmar SMs at this stage of HR maturity.
EXP-04 — 360 Feedback Pilot: G4Closed
Why it closed41% completion rate against an 80% criterion. The 360 process had 14 steps and required 6 raters per G4. Laos SMs found it overwhelming and deprioritised it during field operations season.
Learning applied in
Informed the decision to design the G4 Coaching Quality Scorecard (EXP-13) as a HRBP-led assessment rather than a 360. Removed the SM complexity burden entirely.
EXP-02 — Automated Payroll Variance AlertClosed
Why it closedThe experiment required HRIS integration that was not yet available. The manual workaround was too time-intensive for HR Ops to sustain alongside normal operations.
Learning applied in
Logged as a future experiment for 2027 when the HRIS upgrade is planned. The learning: do not run experiments that depend on infrastructure that does not yet exist.
