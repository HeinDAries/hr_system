C&B Tool 2 — Job Evaluation and Role Grading

JOB EVAL
AND GRADING

A systematic method for determining the relative value of each role at BaKuu Holding — independent of who holds it. Grades are the architecture that salary bands (Tool 3) are built upon.

What Job Evaluation Does
Job evaluation answers the question: how much is this role worth relative to other roles in the company? It scores each role against defined factors — not what the market pays, and not what the current incumbent earns. The score determines the grade. The grade anchors the salary band.

This separates the role from the person. A Site Manager in Laos and a Site Manager in Indonesia should score similarly — even if the salary amounts differ due to local market conditions.
Why BaKuu Holding Needs This
At 28 people across 4 countries, salary decisions are still made largely case-by-case. This works until it does not — until two employees with similar roles compare notes, or a manager promises an increase HR cannot justify, or a new hire negotiates above what existing staff earn for the same work.

A grading system creates the internal logic that makes every compensation decision defensible. It does not remove HR judgment — it gives HR the framework to explain that judgment clearly.
The BaKuu Holding Point-Factor Method
BaKuu Holding uses a simplified point-factor method — the most widely used JE methodology globally, adapted for a lean multi-country mineral exploration operation. Four factors are scored 1–5. Each factor is weighted by importance. The total weighted score maps to a grade (G1–G8).

02 — Evaluation Factors

EVAL
FACTORS

Four factors, each scored 1–5. Use the level anchors to score roles. Score the role requirements — not the person currently in the role.

Critical Rule — Evaluate the Role, Not the PersonA role held by an exceptional person who goes far beyond what the job requires is still evaluated on what the job requires. Individual performance is recognised through position within the salary band, not by inflating the grade.
Factor A — Operational Impact
30%
The degree to which the role directly affects exploration programme delivery, site safety, revenue, or regulatory compliance. Measured by the consequence of a vacancy or poor performance.
1
Minimal impact. Tasks are administrative. Vacancy absorbed by others with little disruption.
2
Some impact on team function. Vacancy creates inconvenience but not operational risk.
3
Meaningful impact on departmental output or compliance. Vacancy requires cover and creates moderate risk.
4
Direct impact on site operations, safety, or multi-country compliance. Vacancy creates immediate operational risk. Hard to cover quickly.
5
Controls critical operational function. Vacancy means immediate site shutdown risk, safety incident risk, or major compliance failure.
Factor B — Technical Expertise
25%
The depth of technical knowledge, formal qualifications, and specialist experience the role requires to be performed competently. Assessed on the minimum viable profile.
1
General secondary education sufficient. On-the-job training develops full capability within weeks.
2
Vocational qualification or 1–2 years relevant experience. Moderate training period to reach full competence.
3
Relevant technical qualification or 3–5 years specialist experience. Role-specific certifications required or strongly expected.
4
Degree-level or professional qualification plus 5+ years specialist experience. Industry certifications mandatory.
5
Advanced or multiple qualifications plus 8+ years specialist experience. Recognised expert-level technical knowledge.
Factor C — Decision Authority
25%
The level of autonomy the role exercises, the nature and consequence of decisions made, and the accountability held for outcomes. Higher scores reflect decisions with wider consequences.
1
Follows defined procedures with close supervision. Decisions are routine and easily reversed.
2
Some discretion within defined parameters. Routine decisions made independently. Work checked regularly.
3
Independent decision-making on day-to-day matters. Consults upward for significant situations. Accountable for own output.
4
Broad autonomy within strategic direction set by senior leadership. Decisions with meaningful financial, safety, or people consequences.
5
Defines strategy and direction for own domain. Final decision authority. Accountable to CEO or Board.
Factor D — People and Stakeholder
20%
The scope and complexity of people management responsibilities and external stakeholder relationships required. Both dimensions contribute to the score.
1
No people management. Stakeholder interaction is internal and routine within own team.
2
Informal guidance of 1–2 junior colleagues. Internal stakeholders across teams. Limited external contact.
3
Manages a small team (3–8 people) or manages complex stakeholder relationships (vendors, authorities, clients).
4
Manages 8+ people or manages critical external relationships (government, regulators, major clients) across multiple countries.
5
Leads a function or multiple teams. Represents the company externally at senior level. Complex multi-country people ecosystems.

07 — Appeals and Reviews

APPEALS
AND REVIEWS

The process for when an employee or manager believes a grade assignment is incorrect. A fair and documented appeals process is essential for the credibility of the JE system.

Valid and Invalid Grounds for Appeal
Valid Grounds
OKRole content has materially changed since last evaluation and the change was not considered
OKA factual error was made in the factor scoring (e.g. a mandatory qualification was overlooked)
OKAn analogous role has been graded differently with no documented rationale
Not Valid Grounds
NoEmployee feels personal contribution deserves higher grade (recognised within band, not by changing grade)
NoCurrent salary is already above what the new grade band would suggest
NoA colleague in a different company earns more for a similar title
The Appeals Process — 4 Steps
1
Written Request to HR
Employee or manager submits written appeal within 30 days of grade being communicated. Must state grounds and provide supporting evidence. Verbal appeals are not accepted.
2
HR Review — 10 Working Days
HR reviews the original evaluation, appeal grounds, and new evidence. May request a meeting with employee and manager. Prepares a written response.
3
Outcome: Upheld or Not Upheld
If upheld: grade revised, salary band updated, retrospective adjustment considered. If not upheld: written explanation with factor-by-factor rationale.
4
Escalation to COO (Final)
If still unsatisfied after HR review, employee may escalate to COO for a final binding decision. No further appeal within the JE process.
