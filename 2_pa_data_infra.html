People Analytics · Tool 2 / 9 — HR Data Infrastructure

HR Data
Infrastructure

Every analytics output is only as good as the data underneath it. This tool defines the infrastructure that makes Valentis people analytics trustworthy: agreed definitions, clear architecture, named ownership, and quality standards that hold at 42 staff and scale to 1,000.

The Infrastructure Principle
Bad data produces confident wrong answers. The COO stops trusting HR analytics after one confident wrong answer.
Data infrastructure is not glamorous work. It does not produce dashboards the COO can point to in a meeting. But it determines whether every other analytics investment pays off. At Valentis, the challenge is not complexity — it is consistency. The pipeline data exists. The salary data exists. The delivery data exists. None of it is defined the same way, collected on the same schedule, or owned by the same person. This tool fixes that.

Five Data Principles at Valentis
One definition, everywhere, permanently
Every term has exactly one definition. Changes require formal governance — not a quiet workaround in a spreadsheet formula.
Every data source has exactly one owner
Shared ownership is no ownership. One named individual is responsible for accuracy and update frequency per source.
Date and format standards are non-negotiable
ISO 8601 dates. Consistent country codes and grade codes. No local variants. Format inconsistency is the most common cause of data join failures in multi-country systems.
Acknowledge gaps — never fill them with estimates presented as facts
Missing data is NULL, not a guess. Estimates are labelled as estimates. Trust is built on honesty about data limitations.
Build for HRIS integration from day one
Every field name and definition is chosen as if it will be migrated into an HRIS at 65–80 staff. The cost of HRIS-compatible structures now is zero. The cost of migrating incompatible data at scale is significant.

ACTIVE_EMPLOYEEAn employee with a current, active employment contract at Valentis…▼
DefinitionAn employee with a current, active employment contract at Valentis Group who is expected to perform work in the current month. Includes employees on approved paid leave. Excludes employees on unpaid leave of more than 30 days, employees under notice (from notification date), and contractors.
Measured onLast working day of each calendar month (snapshot date). Always the same date for all countries.
Used inHeadcount reporting, attrition rate denominator, people cost % denominator, grade distribution.
ExclusionsDo NOT include: contractors, labour hire, secondees from third parties, employees under notice. These may be tracked separately as "extended workforce" but are never included in the active employee count without explicit labelling.
HRIS field nameemployment_status = 'ACTIVE'
HEADCOUNTThe count of active employees at the snapshot date, segmented by grade and country…▼
DefinitionThe count of active employees at the snapshot date (last working day of the month), segmented by grade and country. Headcount is always a whole number — no FTE weighting unless explicitly stated as "FTE headcount."
Standard segmentsBy grade: G1, G2, G3, G4, G5, G6, G7, G8. By country: LA (Laos), MM (Myanmar), ID (Indonesia), TH (Thailand). Country 5 to be added when deployed.
CalculationCOUNT of employees where employment_status = 'ACTIVE' on snapshot date. GROUP BY grade_code, country_code.
Variance notesWhen headcount is reported in a COO brief for a period (e.g. "Q1 2026 headcount"), it refers to the end-of-quarter snapshot unless explicitly stated as an average. Always specify which snapshot date is being used when reporting a single headcount figure.
ATTRITION_RATEThe percentage of the workforce that left Valentis in a given period…▼
DefinitionThe percentage of the workforce that left Valentis in a given period, calculated as: (Number of leavers in period ÷ Average headcount for period) × 100.
Monthly formulaMonthly attrition % = (Leavers in month ÷ ((Opening headcount + Closing headcount) ÷ 2)) × 100
Annual formulaAnnual attrition % = (Total leavers in 12-month period ÷ Average monthly headcount for that 12-month period) × 100. Do NOT simply sum 12 monthly rates.
Rolling 12-monthFor trend reporting: sum of leavers in the trailing 12 months ÷ average headcount over that 12-month period. This is the preferred attrition metric for COO reporting as it smooths seasonal variation.
Inclusion/exclusionINCLUDE: All voluntary resignations, involuntary terminations, retirements, end of contract (if employee was in active employee count). EXCLUDE: Internal transfers between countries (these are tracked separately as "mobility").
Sub-typesVoluntary attrition: employee-initiated. Involuntary attrition: company-initiated (performance, redundancy). Retirement: retirement-initiated. Report all three separately — combined figure is "total attrition."
TENURELength of continuous employment at Valentis from hire date to snapshot date…▼
DefinitionLength of continuous employment at Valentis from hire_date to snapshot date (or exit_date for leavers). Measured in whole months for reporting purposes (round down to nearest month).
CalculationFLOOR(MONTHS_BETWEEN(snapshot_date, hire_date))
Bands for reporting0–6 months (new hire), 6–12 months, 12–24 months, 24–36 months, 36–60 months, 60+ months (long-tenured). These bands align with milestone progression stages.
Note on continuityIf an employee exits and is rehired, tenure restarts from the new hire date unless a specific continuity agreement is documented. Do not combine pre-exit and post-rehire tenure without explicit business reason.
PEOPLE_COST_RATIOTotal people cost as a percentage of total revenue for the period…▼
DefinitionTotal people cost as a percentage of total revenue for the period. People cost includes: base salaries, allowances, overtime, employer statutory contributions, L&D investment, and recruitment costs. Excludes: contractor fees (reported separately as "extended workforce cost"), one-off severance unless noted.
FormulaPeople Cost Ratio = (Total people cost for period ÷ Total revenue for period) × 100. Valentis baseline: approximately 43%.
Reporting frequencyMonthly from payroll + finance P&L. Quarterly trend in COO report. Annual benchmark comparison.
Alert thresholdFlag to COO if people cost ratio exceeds 47% in any month or 45% on a rolling 3-month basis. Investigate driver before next quarterly report.
PIPELINE_CANDIDATEAn active employee currently progressing through the G1→G4 driller development pathway…▼
DefinitionAn active employee currently progressing through the G1→G4 driller development pathway. Includes G1, G2, and G3 employees who have not yet been confirmed as G4 and have not exited the pipeline. Does NOT include G4+ employees who have completed the pipeline.
Pipeline stagesG1 Trainee (intake to G2 confirmation), G2 Driller's Assistant (G2 confirmation to G3 confirmation), G3 Junior Driller (G3 confirmation to G4 confirmation). Each stage has defined milestones in the sign-off card system.
Status categoriesON_TRACK: progressing at expected velocity. AT_RISK: milestone overdue by more than 60 days or check-in flagged. STALLED: no milestone progress in 90+ days. EXITED: left pipeline voluntarily or involuntarily before G4.
CONVERSION_RATEThe percentage of pipeline candidates entering at G1 who achieve G4 confirmation…▼
DefinitionThe percentage of pipeline candidates entering at G1 who achieve G4 confirmation. Calculated as: (Number of G4 confirmations in period ÷ Number of G1 intakes in the same cohort period) × 100. Cohort-based — not period-based.
Cohort definitionA cohort is a group of G1 intakes within the same calendar quarter. Cohort conversion rate is only calculable after 54–66 months (the full G1→G4 pathway duration). For current reporting, use "stage conversion rate" as the proxy.
Stage conversion ratesG1→G2: (G2 confirmations in period ÷ G1 intakes 18–24 months prior) × 100. G2→G3: (G3 confirmations ÷ G2 confirmations 12–18 months prior) × 100. G3→G4: (G4 confirmations ÷ G3 confirmations 18–24 months prior) × 100.
TargetOverall G1→G4 conversion: ≥70%. Current Myanmar baseline: ~58%. Laos and Indonesia: establish baseline Q1 2026.
Country-specific noteConversion rates are always reported by country. Do not report a group-wide conversion rate without also showing the country breakdown — country mix drives the aggregate and can mask individual country deterioration.
TIME_IN_GRADEThe number of months an employee has held their current grade…▼
DefinitionThe number of months an employee has held their current grade, measured from grade_start_date to snapshot_date. Distinct from tenure (total time at Valentis) — an employee may have 36 months tenure but only 8 months time-in-grade if recently promoted.
CalculationFLOOR(MONTHS_BETWEEN(snapshot_date, grade_start_date))
Target rangesG1: 18–24 months before G2 confirmation. G2: 12–18 months. G3: 18–24 months. Extended time-in-grade (G3 at 30+ months without G4 confirmation) triggers a pipeline stall review.
Alert ruleFlag any G3 with time-in-grade >28 months who has not yet received G4 confirmation as AT_RISK in the pipeline log. Trigger Group HR review within 30 days.
MILESTONE_COMPLETIONA formal sign-off by an authorised G4 or SM confirming an employee has demonstrated competency…▼
DefinitionA formal sign-off by an authorised G4 or SM confirming an employee has demonstrated competency at a defined milestone standard. A milestone is complete only when the physical sign-off card is signed by an authorised signatory. Verbal confirmation, email, or WhatsApp message does not constitute milestone completion.
Required signatoriesG1→G2 milestones: G4 signature. G2→G3 milestones: G4 signature + SM countersignature. G3→G4 milestones: SM signature + Group HR notification.
RecordingMilestone completion date (YYYY-MM-DD), milestone code, signatory ID, and any qualitative notes are recorded in the pipeline log within 7 days of sign-off. Late recording is flagged in the monthly data quality review.
BENCH_STRENGTHThe number of G3 candidates who are within 12 months of G4 confirmation…▼
DefinitionThe number of G3 candidates who are within 12 months of G4 confirmation and have been assessed as ON_TRACK by their SM. Bench strength represents the near-term G4 supply — the leading indicator of operational capacity 12 months out.
CalculationCOUNT of G3 employees where: (a) time_in_grade is between 12 and 24 months, (b) pipeline_status = 'ON_TRACK', and (c) SM has assessed as G4-ready within 12 months. Reported by country.
TargetMinimum bench strength: 1 G3 candidate per active country at any time. Laos critical risk: only 2 G3 candidates against 5 G4 positions, 3 of which are retirement-risk within 3 years.
BASE_SALARYThe fixed annual or monthly compensation paid to an employee, excluding all variable pay…▼
DefinitionThe fixed annual or monthly compensation paid to an employee, excluding all variable pay (bonuses, incentives, allowances). This is the number used for grade band adherence analysis and pay equity review. Always expressed in USD for cross-country comparison — local currency in payroll records, USD equivalent for analytics.
USD conversionConvert using the exchange rate on the first working day of January each year (the annual rate). Do not use daily spot rates for compensation analytics — this creates artificial variance. The annual rate is documented in the compensation analytics record each January.
Excluded from base salarySite allowance, housing allowance, transport allowance, per diem, overtime, performance bonus, retention bonus, statutory contributions (employer-side), and benefits in kind. These are tracked separately as "total cash" and "total remuneration."
COMPA_RATIOAn employee's base salary as a percentage of the midpoint of their grade band…▼
DefinitionAn employee's base salary as a percentage of the midpoint of their grade band. Compa-ratio = (Employee base salary ÷ Grade band midpoint) × 100. A compa-ratio of 100 means the employee is paid exactly at midpoint.
InterpretationBelow 85: significantly below midpoint — possible below-minimum risk, investigate against band floor. 85–95: below midpoint, typically new to grade. 95–105: at midpoint, fully competent in role. 105–115: above midpoint, high performer or long-tenured. Above 115: above midpoint — flag for merit review justification.
Country factor applicationGrade bands are expressed in USD base rates. Country factors (LA: 85%, MM: 80%, ID: 92%, TH: 105%) adjust the band floor, midpoint, and ceiling for each country. Compa-ratio always uses the country-adjusted midpoint, not the USD base midpoint.
BAND_ADHERENCEThe percentage of employees whose base salary falls within the floor and ceiling of their grade band…▼
DefinitionThe percentage of employees whose base salary (after country factor adjustment) falls within the floor and ceiling of their grade band. Band_Adherence = (Employees within band ÷ Total employees) × 100. Target: ≥95%.
Below-minimumAny employee whose base salary is below the grade band floor (country-adjusted) is a below-minimum case. These must be flagged to the COO within 30 days of identification and remediated within the next payroll cycle unless a formal exception is documented and approved.
Live caseZaw M. (Myanmar, G3): confirmed below-minimum. Action: salary adjustment to band floor by April 2026 payroll. Documented exception until that date.

Section 05 — Data Quality Standards

Data Quality
Standards

Quality standards define what "good data" looks like at Valentis — and what triggers a quality alert. Three dimensions: completeness (are all required fields populated?), accuracy (is the data correct?), and timeliness (is the data current?). Standards are set per data source and reviewed quarterly.

Quality Dimensions
COMPLETENESS
Are all required fields populated?
Completeness is measured as: (Fields populated ÷ Required fields) × 100. Target: 100% for all required fields in the dictionary. A completeness rate below 95% for any required field in any quarter is a quality alert — Group HR identifies and closes the gap before the next COO report.
ACCURACY
Is the data correct and consistent?
Accuracy is harder to measure automatically — it requires cross-validation. Key accuracy checks: headcount in HR Employee Register matches payroll headcount (monthly reconciliation). Pipeline log milestone counts plausible for time-in-grade (stage conversion velocity check). Salary vs band: compa-ratios within expected range (outlier review).
TIMELINESS
Is the data current?
Each data source has a defined update schedule (Section 04). Timeliness is measured as: data age vs scheduled update frequency. A data source that is more than one update cycle out of date is a timeliness alert. Stale data used in a COO report must be labelled with its data date.

Data Protection Rules at Valentis
At 42 staff across four countries, HR data protection is a matter of professional practice rather than complex policy. These rules apply to all HR data regardless of country.
Individual salary data is strictly confidentialAn employee's salary, compa-ratio, and position in band is known only to Group HR and the COO. SMs do not have access to salary data for their team members unless the COO has specifically approved this. Analytics shared with SMs always uses grade-level aggregates, never individual salary data.
Check-in risk ratings are confidential to Group HR and the relevant G4/SMA trainee's check-in risk level is not shared beyond the G4 who conducted it, the SM, and Group HR. It is never included in a COO report with individual identification. Pipeline analytics uses risk level only in aggregate (e.g. "2 of 8 trainees rated HIGH risk in Myanmar Q1 2026").
HR data files are not shared via unencrypted channelsHR data files (employee register, pipeline log, salary extract) are not shared via WhatsApp or unencrypted email. When sharing is necessary, use password-protected files or the company's designated document storage system. This applies especially to cross-border sharing between countries.

Section 07 — Employee ID System

Employee
ID System

A structured unique employee ID is the joining key that links data across every HR system — payroll, pipeline log, check-in records, coaching data, compensation records. Without a consistent ID, every cross-system analysis requires manual name matching. This section defines the Valentis ID format, assignment rules, and the backfill plan for current employees.

ID Format
[Country Code] – [Grade Code] – [4-digit Sequence]
COUNTRY CODE
CC
LA (Laos) · MM (Myanmar) · ID (Indonesia) · TH (Thailand). Country code reflects primary work location at time of hire. Does not change if employee transfers country — a new record is created for a permanent transfer.
GRADE CODE AT HIRE
GG
G1 through G8. Grade code in the ID reflects the grade at hire — it does NOT change when the employee is promoted. The ID is permanent. Current grade is tracked in the employee record, not the ID.
SEQUENCE NUMBER
NNNN
4-digit zero-padded sequence number within the country-grade combination. Starts at 0001. Increments with each new hire in that country-grade combination. Never reused — retired employee IDs are never reassigned.

ID Assignment Rules
Assign at hire: The employee ID is assigned on the first day of employment. It appears in the offer letter and employment contract. It is used in every HR system from day one.
Never reuse retired IDs: When an employee exits, their ID is retired. The sequence number is never reused. This prevents data from a former employee being accidentally merged with a new hire's record.
Rehires get a new ID: If an employee exits and is later rehired, they receive a new employee ID. The old ID is cross-referenced in the notes field. Tenure restarts from the new hire date.
Country transfers: A permanent transfer between countries generates a new employee ID with the new country code. The old ID is cross-referenced. Continuity of service is documented separately — it does not change the ID.
Group HR controls the sequence register: Group HR maintains a sequence register per country-grade combination. This is the source of truth for the next available ID number. It is updated every time an ID is assigned and reviewed in the quarterly data quality check.
Backfill plan for current employees: All current active employees are assigned an ID in Q1 2026 as part of the data infrastructure build. Group HR uses the payroll file and SM records to reconstruct hire grade and approximate hire sequence for the backfill. Backfilled IDs are flagged with a "backfilled" note in the register — they carry the same validity as newly assigned IDs going forward.
