Scope Note
BaKuu Holding operates in 4 SE Asian jurisdictions each with distinct data protection frameworks at varying stages of maturity. Thailand's PDPA (effective 2022) is the most comprehensive. Indonesia's PDP Law (2024) is the newest. Laos and Myanmar have limited formal data protection legislation — compliance defaults to international best practice and group HR policy.
Retention Principle
Retain only what is necessary for the longest applicable legal obligation across all 4 countries. Where laws conflict, apply the longer period.
Current Gap
No formal deletion schedule exists. Employee data is retained indefinitely in Excel HRIS. This creates unnecessary risk for former employees' personal data.
Action Required
Implement deletion / anonymisation schedule for former employees. Review and purge records older than applicable retention periods. Assign quarterly review cadence to Group HR Manager.
🚨
Data Breach Response — Time-Critical
Thailand PDPA requires notification to the PDPC within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach. Indonesia PDP Law requires notification within 14 days. Laos and Myanmar: notify affected individuals and escalate to legal counsel. Time starts from the moment of discovery — not the moment the breach occurred.

Lawful Basis Framework
Most HR data processing at BaKuu Holding is lawful under contractual necessity (data needed to perform the employment contract) or legal obligation (data required by local labour law). Consent should only be relied upon where no other basis applies — it can be withdrawn, creating operational risk.
