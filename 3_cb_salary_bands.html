C&B Tool 3 — Salary Band Management

Salary
Band Management

Eight salary bands, one per grade. Each band defines the minimum, midpoint, and maximum pay for roles at that level. Bands translate the JE grade (Tool 2) into an actual salary range anchored to market data.

Band Anatomy
Every salary band has three reference points. Together they define the full shape of the band and how employees move through it.
MIN
The floor of the band. Entry point for new hires or employees new to the role. No employee in this grade should be paid below this figure.
MID
The market reference point — where a fully competent employee performing at expectations (Rating 3) should sit. Compa-ratio of 100 = at midpoint. This is the anchor for all salary decisions.
MAX
The ceiling of the band. Reached only by long-tenured, consistently high-performing employees. Employees above max are in red-circle status — eligible for bonuses only, not base increases, until the band is revised.
Band Design Principles
Band Spread
BaKuu Holding uses a 50% spread — minimum is 80% of midpoint, maximum is 120% of midpoint. This creates a band where the max is 50% above the min. Wider than standard for operational roles to accommodate long-tenure site staff.
Band Overlap
Adjacent bands overlap by approximately 15–20%. This means a high-performing G4 employee can earn more than a newly appointed G5 employee — and that is intentional. Overlap reflects experience and performance, not just grade.
Annual Review
Bands are reviewed every Q4 against updated market benchmarks. Bands typically increase by 2–5% annually in line with market movement. Band adjustments are independent of individual merit increases.

Bands

USD Reference OnlyThese figures represent USD purchasing power equivalents for benchmarking purposes. They are not paid in USD to local staff — local currency equivalents apply (Section 03). Expatriate or cross-border contracts may be denominated in USD by agreement.

Tables

Section 05 — Internal Equity

Internal Equity
Analysis

Internal equity analysis checks whether employees in the same grade are being paid consistently, and whether any patterns exist that cannot be justified by performance, tenure, or market differences.

What equity analysis is notIt is not about making everyone in the same grade earn the same salary. It is about ensuring that differences within a grade can be explained by legitimate factors: performance rating, tenure, country, and position in band. Unexplained differences are the problem.
Compa-Ratio Distribution — All Employees
Current compa-ratio spread across BaKuu Holding. Healthy distribution should cluster around 95–105% with a long tail toward higher values for tenured staff.
Equity Red Flags to Investigate
!
Same role, same country, 15%+ salary difference with no documented justification
Most commonly found when a new hire negotiated a higher package than the existing employee. Requires written explanation or remediation plan.
!
Long-tenured employee below midpoint with consistently good ratings
Indicates merit increases have not kept pace. This employee is likely a flight risk and may be unaware of how far below market they are. Requires a catch-up plan.
!
New hire paid above the midpoint with no market justification
Compresses the band for existing employees and signals that salary decisions are being driven by negotiation rather than methodology. Requires written exception approval.
~
All employees in a grade clustered near the maximum
May signal the grade is too low for the actual market value of the role, or that merit increases have been too generous without differentiation. Review grade assignment and market data.

Section 06 — Band Review

Band Review
Process

The annual process for updating salary bands to keep pace with market movement. Conducted every Q4 before the merit cycle. A well-run band review takes 2–3 weeks.

Band Review Timeline — Q4 Annual Process
October
Wk 1–2
Collect Market Data
Pull latest salary survey data for mining and oil and gas sector in SEA. Reference sources: Mercer, Korn Ferry, Willis Towers Watson regional surveys, or equivalent. Minimum 2 sources per country.
October
Wk 3–4
Benchmark Each Grade
Compare current BaKuu Holding band midpoints to market P50 for each grade. Calculate the gap as a percentage. Flag any grade where the gap exceeds 8% — these require a band adjustment.
November
Wk 1
Propose Band Adjustments
HR prepares a band adjustment proposal for each grade needing revision. Include: current midpoint, proposed midpoint, market data source, and estimated cost impact of any employees now below the new minimum.
November
Wk 2
COO and CEO Approval
HR presents band review to COO. CEO approves final changes. Any employee below the new minimum must be brought up to minimum — this is a mandatory cost that must be budgeted.
December
Publish New Bands
Updated bands take effect 1 January. HR updates all band reference documents. Managers are briefed before the merit cycle begins. Employees are not told of band changes — only of their own salary changes.

Section 07 — Out-of-Band Employees

Out-of-Band
Employees

Employees paid outside their salary band — either below minimum (rare, requires immediate action) or above maximum (red circle status — managed carefully). Both require a documented plan.

Below Band Minimum
Any employee paid below the band minimum is in breach of the salary band policy. This is the highest priority correction.
CauseUsually: band was raised but the employee was not brought up, or a legacy hire who joined below the then-minimum.
ActionBring to band minimum at the next payroll cycle. This is not discretionary. Document the correction in the HR file.
EscalationNotify COO. Investigate why the gap occurred. Update internal controls to prevent recurrence.
Above Band Maximum (Red Circle)
An employee paid above the band maximum. More common — usually occurs when bands are adjusted downward or a long-tenured high performer has been progressed to the ceiling over many years.
Do Not Cut Their SalaryRed circle employees retain their current salary. Salary reductions are almost never acceptable and create serious legal and morale risk.
Freeze Base IncreasesNo merit increases to base salary until the band maximum moves above their current salary. Recognise performance through annual bonus only.
Review GradeIf the employee is consistently above max, consider whether the grade assignment is correct. Their role may have grown beyond the current grade.
