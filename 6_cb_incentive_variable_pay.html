C&B Tool 6 — Incentive and Variable Pay Framework

Incentive &
Variable Pay

The framework that governs all forms of performance-linked and discretionary variable pay at BaKuu Holding — annual bonuses, site and project incentives, and retention awards. Separate from and complementary to the merit system in Tool 4.

Variable Pay vs Merit Increase
These are two distinct instruments that should never be confused — or conflated in a conversation with an employee.

Merit increase (Tool 4) is a permanent, compounding change to base salary. It recognises sustained performance over the year. Once given, it is never taken back.

Variable pay (this tool) is a one-time payment that does not compound. It recognises outcomes achieved in a specific period. It can be zero the following year even if performance was excellent — because outcomes vary.

Using variable pay correctly means reserving it for genuine performance differentiation and outcome-linked results — not as a supplement for a salary that is too low, and not as an apology for a low merit increase.
BaKuu Holding Variable Pay Philosophy
Line of Sight
Every employee should be able to trace a direct line from their day-to-day work to their incentive outcome. Vague or unmeasurable KPIs make variable pay feel arbitrary rather than earned.
At Risk, Not Guaranteed
Variable pay must be genuinely at risk. If it is paid every year at full target regardless of performance, it becomes a de facto base salary — and erodes both its motivational value and the company budget.
Approved Before Communicated
No bonus amount, retention figure, or incentive award is communicated to an employee before it has been approved at the required authority level. Verbal commitments before approval are a governance failure.
Documented and Auditable
Every variable pay award has a paper trail: KPI performance data, calculation workings, approval record, and signed letter. No undocumented discretionary payments.
BaKuu Holding Variable Pay Architecture — 2026
Three incentive schemes serve different purposes and different employee populations. They can co-exist — a site employee may be eligible for both the annual bonus and a site incentive simultaneously.
🏆
Annual Performance Bonus
All eligible employees
Linked to individual PM rating plus company performance gate. Paid annually in January. Target bonus expressed as a percentage of base salary by grade.
⛏
Site and Project Incentive
Operational site staff
Linked to specific site or project operational milestones. Paid on milestone achievement, not annually. Safety performance is always a gate condition.

Awarded to retain employees in roles with no short-term successor and high market demand. Structured as a clawback arrangement with defined service periods.

Section 03 — KPI Framework

KPI
Framework

The KPIs that drive variable pay must be set before the performance period begins, agreed with the employee, and measurable without ambiguity. These are the standard KPI categories for BaKuu Holding incentive schemes.

KPI Design PrincipleA KPI that cannot be objectively measured at year-end is not a valid incentive KPI. If the assessment of "did they achieve this?" requires significant management judgement rather than data, it belongs in the qualitative PM assessment — not in a variable pay formula.

Section 05 — Retention Bonus

Retention
Bonus

The most carefully governed variable pay instrument at BaKuu Holding. Used sparingly and deliberately for roles where loss of the incumbent would create immediate operational risk. CEO approval is always required.

This is not a reward — it is an insurance policyA retention bonus is not recognition of strong performance — that is handled through merit increases and the annual bonus. A retention bonus is a calculated business decision to secure continuity in a critical role. Framing it as a reward risks creating expectations across the workforce.
Retention Bonus Qualification Criteria
All four criteria must be met. A retention bonus should not be considered unless the HR case clearly satisfies every gate.
Retention Bonus Structures
Structure A — Deferred Lump Sum
Full amount paid at the end of the service period. Simplest structure. Employee receives nothing early. Full clawback applies if they resign before the period ends.
Best for: Short service periods (12 months)
Structure B — Split Payment (50/50)
50% paid on award. 50% paid at the end of the service period. Clawback applies to both tranches if the employee resigns before the full period.
Best for: Longer periods (18–24 months)
Structure C — Milestone-Linked
Payment linked to specific operational milestones (e.g. programme completion, successor development). Used where a time-based service period is less relevant than a specific outcome.
Best for: Project-based critical roles
Retention Bonus — Clawback Rules
Voluntary ResignationFull clawback of all amounts paid under the retention agreement. Employee must repay within 60 days of resignation.
Company-Initiated RedundancyNo clawback. If BaKuu Holding eliminates the role, the employee retains all amounts received. The retention agreement is voided.
Performance-Based TerminationClawback of amounts paid in the 12 months before termination. Legal advice required before initiating — local employment law varies significantly.
Mutual Agreement / MedicalCOO discretion. Clawback waived or negotiated on a case-by-case basis with legal review.
Key RequirementClawback provisions must be clearly written into the signed retention agreement before any payment is made. Verbal clawback agreements are not enforceable.

Section 06 — Governance and Approval

Governance
and Approval

Approval authority for every variable pay decision. No payment is made without the required sign-off. HR is responsible for ensuring the governance trail is complete before any amount is communicated to an employee.

Approval Authority by Scheme and Amount
Annual Bonus Cycle — Governance Timeline
Nov Wk 3
HR presents KPI achievement data to COO
Company gate performance against programme target. LTIFR and safety data. Per-employee performance ratings from calibrated PM cycle.
Nov Wk 4
COO recommends company gate outcome
COO confirms gate pass/partial/fail. Any adjustments to the gate threshold require written rationale. HR calculates total bonus cost under COO recommendation.
Dec Wk 1
CEO approves total bonus pool and individual awards
CEO reviews total cost, individual amounts, and any exceptions. CEO approval is final. No bonus is paid without CEO sign-off on the total award schedule.
Dec Wk 2
Bonus letters generated and signed
HR generates individual bonus letters (Section 08). Letters are signed by the employee before payment is processed. No unsigned letter = no payment.
January
Payment processed with January payroll
Bonus paid with the January payroll run. Payroll team receives approved schedule from HR — not from managers. Tax is withheld at source per country requirements.
The Golden Rule of Variable Pay GovernanceA manager who communicates a bonus amount to an employee before it has been approved by the required authority has created a legal and financial liability for BaKuu Holding. The employee now has a reasonable expectation of that amount even if the formal approval is lower. HR must be the sole communication channel until approval is complete.
Section 07 — Communication Guide

Communication
Guide

Scripts and guidance for delivering variable pay outcomes. The goal is always the same: the employee understands what they are receiving, why, and how it was calculated — with no surprises and no unanswerable questions.

Script 1 — Strong Bonus Outcome (Gate Passed, Rating 4–5)
"I want to share your bonus outcome for this year. The company met its programme target for 2026, which means the company performance gate is fully open. Your individual performance this year was rated [4/5], which applies a [1.3/1.6] multiplier to your target bonus.

Your bonus this year is [amount]. You will receive this with your January payroll. A letter confirming the amount and the calculation is ready for your signature — please review it and let me know if you have any questions about how we arrived at the figure.

I want to be clear that this is a genuine reflection of your contribution this year. Thank you for what you delivered."
Note: Always have the signed letter before the conversation if possible. Never share the amount verbally before the letter is ready.
Script 2 — Gate Failed or Partial (Lower or Zero Bonus)
"I need to talk with you about this year's bonus outcome. The company did not meet the full programme target in 2026 — we achieved [X]% of the drilling metres we set as our gate target. Under the bonus framework, this means the company gate is [partially open / closed].

Your individual performance rating is [X], which I want you to know I consider a strong result on your part. But the variable pay framework is designed so that individual performance alone cannot generate a full bonus when the company did not deliver its programme. Your bonus this year is [amount / zero].

I understand that may be disappointing. I want to be clear that your performance has been fully recognised in your merit increase — the bonus outcome is a reflection of the programme result, not of your contribution."
Note: This is the most difficult bonus conversation. Prepare for the natural reaction that the employee feels unfairly treated. Have the calculation workings available if they want to understand the maths.
Script 3 — Retention Bonus Discussion
"I want to have a confidential conversation with you. The company has decided to offer you a retention arrangement, and I want to explain what that means before you make any decision.

We value your role in the business significantly, and we want to make a formal commitment to support you staying with us. The arrangement is [amount], structured as [Structure A/B/C]. In exchange, we are asking for a commitment of [X months]. There is a full repayment obligation if you choose to leave within that period — that detail is in the agreement I have here.

This is not an obligation — you are free to review it and come back to me. I would ask that you keep this arrangement confidential as it is an individual arrangement between you and the company."
Note: Allow the employee time to read the agreement before signing. Never pressure for same-day signature. Always have legal review the agreement before this conversation.
Frequently Asked Questions — Variable Pay
"Why is my bonus lower than last year if I performed better?"
The annual bonus depends on both your performance and the company gate result. If the company programme performance was lower this year than last year, the gate factor reduces the total payout regardless of individual rating. This is by design — variable pay reflects collective outcomes, not just individual ones.
"What is the maximum bonus I can receive?"
The maximum is the target bonus percentage for your grade multiplied by the maximum performance multiplier (1.6x for a Rating 5) when the company gate is fully passed. For a G4 employee, that is 15% x 1.6 = 24% of base salary. This can be shown in the bonus calculator (Section 04).
"My manager told me I would get a certain bonus amount — why is it different?"
Managers are not authorised to commit to specific bonus amounts. Only HR can communicate approved amounts, and only after CEO sign-off. If a manager communicated a figure before approval, please speak with HR — this is a process issue we need to address. The approved amount in your letter is the correct figure.
