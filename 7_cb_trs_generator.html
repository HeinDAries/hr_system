C&B Tool 7 — Total Rewards Statement Generator

Total Rewards
Statement

The statement that makes the full value of employment visible. Most employees only see their base salary. A Total Rewards Statement reveals the complete picture — and changes how people think about what they earn.

What a TRS Does
An employee earning $38,000 base salary at a BaKuu Holding site in Laos is not receiving $38,000 in total value. They are receiving:

Base salary$38,000
Site allowance (G4)$5,400
Annual bonus target$5,700
Medical insurance$2,400
Meals (on-site)$2,736
R&R flights (6 cycles)$2,400
Accommodation$3,600
Total employer value$60,236
That is 59% more than the base salary figure the employee sees on their contract. Without a TRS, they do not know this.
Why This Matters Strategically
Retention
Employees who understand their full total rewards package are significantly less likely to be swayed by a competitor offer that appears higher on base salary alone. The TRS makes the true cost of switching visible.
Recruitment
Attaching a TRS to an offer letter transforms the offer conversation. Candidates who compare only base salaries are shown the complete picture. BaKuu Holding becomes materially more competitive — especially for site roles where cash allowances are significant.
HR Credibility
Producing a TRS demonstrates that BaKuu Holding HR understands and actively manages total compensation — not just payroll. It positions the HR function as a strategic partner, not an administrative one. This is the document that earns a seat at the planning table.
Perceived Fairness
Employees who feel underpaid are often comparing their base salary to an imagined market number. The TRS provides a fact-based anchor for that conversation — and frequently reveals that the total package is more competitive than the employee realised.
The Five Pillars in the TRS
Every Total Rewards Statement at BaKuu Holding uses the five-pillar structure from the Total Rewards Framework (Tool 1). Each pillar contributes to the total value calculation.
💰
Pillar I
BASE PAY
Annual base salary. The fixed, guaranteed component.
🏆

⛏

ALLOWANCES
Site, R&R, meals, accommodation, transport, hardship.
📚
Pillar V
DEVELOPMENT
Training, certification, career investment. Estimated annual value.

Section 04 — Communication Strategy

Communication
Strategy

How and when to deliver the Total Rewards Statement. The document itself is only half the work — the conversation around it determines whether it lands as intended.

When to Issue a TRS
Annual Cycle — Dec
Issue annually alongside the merit letter. Delivered in the same meeting where the merit increase is communicated. This is the primary annual touchpoint for total rewards visibility.
Offer Stage
Attach to an offer letter for senior or hard-to-source roles. For drillers and site supervisors, the total allowance package is often the deciding factor — make it explicit at the offer stage rather than waiting.
Retention Conversation
When an employee raises a competing offer or signals they are considering leaving, produce the TRS immediately. The true cost of their current package is often the most powerful retention tool available before a retention bonus is needed.
Promotion
Issue an updated TRS when promoting an employee. It makes the full value of the promotion visible — not just the salary increase — and reinforces the message that the company is investing in them.
Delivery Script — Annual TRS Meeting
"I want to share something with you today that I think gives you a more complete picture of what you receive from BaKuu Holding. This is your Total Rewards Statement for 2026.

Most of what you see day to day is your base salary — [amount]. But that is only part of what the company invests in you. When we add your bonus target, your medical coverage, your site allowances, R&R flights, accommodation and meals — the total value is actually [total amount].

I want you to have this document because I think it is important that you understand what your package is truly worth. If you ever receive an offer from another employer that looks attractive on salary, I would encourage you to compare the full picture — not just the headline number.

Do you have any questions about anything you see on this statement?"
What the TRS Does NOT Include
Being transparent about what is and is not in the TRS builds trust. Do not overstate the value.
Statutory employer contributions — BPJS, SSF, NSSF etc. These are not included as they flow to the government scheme, not directly to the employee. Including them risks overstating the employee-felt value.
Non-discretionary operational costs — company vehicles, equipment, safety gear, PPE. These are business costs, not personal rewards.
Future earnings or projected increases — the TRS reflects current-year value only. Do not include projected raises or bonus increases.
