BaKuu Holding C&B System · Chapter I

Our Rewards
Philosophy

The Total Rewards Framework is the single source of truth for how BaKuu Holding values, compensates, and rewards its people. Every salary decision, bonus payment, and benefits choice flows from this document.

Why This Framework Exists
Compensation is
not a transaction
In a 28-person company operating across four countries in mineral exploration, every compensation decision is visible, felt, and remembered. There is no anonymity. When two drillers with similar experience discover they are on very different packages, without a clear rationale, the damage to trust and morale is immediate and lasting. This framework exists to ensure that every rewards decision has a defensible, consistent rationale — anchored in market data, internal equity, and business strategy.
1
Pay for the role, recognise the person
The salary band is determined by the job — its complexity, impact, and market value. Where an individual sits within that band reflects their performance, tenure, and demonstrated capability. This distinction is fundamental. Two people in the same role can earn different amounts for legitimate, documented reasons. But both are paid within the same band, governed by the same methodology.
2
Competitive enough to attract, fair enough to retain
BaKuu Holding targets the market median for most roles — the 50th percentile of relevant industry benchmarks. For critical and hard-to-fill roles (Site Managers, experienced Drillers), we target the 60th–75th percentile to remain competitive in a supply-constrained market. We do not try to be the highest payer in the market, but we will not lose talent to a 10% salary gap that we could have closed.
3
Total rewards, not just base pay
Base salary is one component of what BaKuu Holding offers. Site allowances, housing, medical coverage, provident fund contributions, performance bonuses, career development support, and job security in a stable privately-held company — together, these often exceed what a higher-base competitor is offering. We communicate the full value of the package, not just the number on the payslip.
4
Performance must connect to reward
Salary increases, bonuses, and development investment are differentiated by performance. An employee rated 5 should see a materially different reward outcome than an employee rated 3. If everyone gets the same increase regardless of contribution, the PM system loses credibility — and so does the company's commitment to fairness. This connection is built in from the start and is non-negotiable.
5
Country differentiation, not country discrimination
BaKuu Holding operates in four countries with fundamentally different cost of living, labour markets, and statutory requirements. A driller in Laos and a driller in Singapore cannot and should not be on identical packages. Differentiation by country is not discrimination — it is economic reality. What must be consistent is the methodology, the fairness of the process, and the respect with which compensation conversations are conducted in every country.

Pillar I
Base Compensation
The fixed monthly salary, determined by job grade and position within the salary band. This is the foundation of the package — stable, predictable, and benchmarked against the external market.
What it is
Fixed Monthly Salary
Gross base pay, paid monthly in local currency. Determined by job grade (Tool 2) and position within the salary band (Tool 3).
How it is set
Band + Performance Position
New hires enter at 80–95% of band midpoint. Existing employees advance within band via annual merit review linked to PM rating. Moves above midpoint require strong sustained performance.
Who approves changes
HR + COO / CEO
Annual merit increases: HR recommends, COO approves. Off-cycle increases or promotions: HR prepares business case, CEO approves. See Governance section.
⚡
Pillar II
Variable Pay
Performance-linked pay that rewards exceptional contribution, company success, and retention of critical talent. Variable pay is not guaranteed — it is earned through measurable outcomes.
Annual Bonus
Company + Individual
Paid Q1 of following year. Linked to company financial performance and individual PM rating. Only employees rated 3 or above are eligible. Full framework in Tool 6.
Site / Project Incentive
Operational Milestone
Paid when a drilling programme or project achieves defined targets — meterage, timeline, safety record. Specific to operational staff. Defined per-programme by COO.
Retention Bonus
Critical Talent Only
One-time payment for employees identified as critical flight risks. Requires written agreement. Full details in Tool 6. CEO approval required.

Pillar IV
Work Environment & Allowances
Site-based operations require compensation components that go beyond standard office employment. Allowances recognise the conditions, remoteness, and demands of working in active exploration sites. They are a significant part of the total package for operational staff.
Site Allowance
Remote Location Premium
Paid to all employees working on active exploration sites. Reflects remoteness, limited amenities, and separation from family. Rate varies by site designation (defined by COO annually).
Accommodation
Company-Provided or Allowance
Site staff: company camp accommodation provided. Office-based staff in countries without local housing network: accommodation allowance at agreed rate, reviewed annually.
R&R / Rotation
Rotation & Travel
Site staff on rotation schedule receive paid travel to home city at agreed frequency. Rotation schedule defined per site and role — typically 6 weeks on / 2 weeks off for remote sites.
Meals
Company-Provided On-Site
All meals provided on active sites. This is a meaningful component of total compensation that employees often undervalue — include in total rewards statements.
Hardship
Country Hardship Premium
Applies to expatriate or cross-border staff deployed to higher-risk or higher-hardship locations. Myanmar currently carries a hardship designation. Rate: defined by HR and approved by CEO.
Transport
Site Transfer & Commute
Company transport to and from sites for rotational staff. For office-based staff: transport allowance where public transport is inadequate or unavailable (Myanmar, some Laos locations).

Chapter III

Market
Positioning

Where BaKuu Holding chooses to sit relative to the market is a strategic decision — not a default. This chapter defines our positioning by role type and explains the rationale behind each choice.

BaKuu Holding Market Positioning Policy
BaKuu Holding uses external salary surveys (mining and oil & gas sector benchmarks, regional HR surveys) to anchor salary bands to market data. Positioning is reviewed annually in Q4, before the merit cycle begins. The default target position is P50 — but this is adjusted by role criticality.

Compa-Ratio — Reading Your Position in the Band
Compa-ratio compares an employee's salary to the midpoint of their salary band. It is the key metric for understanding whether an individual is paid fairly relative to their market position.
Below 80%
Significantly below market. Priority for increase.
80–95%
New hire range or developing role
95–105%
Fully performing at market. Target zone.
105–120%
High performer, strong tenure
Above 120%
Above band. Review at next cycle.
FormulaCompa-Ratio = (Employee Salary ÷ Band Midpoint) × 100. A compa-ratio of 100 means the employee is paid exactly at the midpoint. Used in the Merit Matrix (Tool 4) to determine merit increase guidelines.
Chapter IV

Country
Differentiation

One methodology, four markets. The same grading framework and philosophy applies in all four countries — but the absolute numbers, statutory requirements, and currency vary significantly.

The PrincipleBaKuu Holding does not set a "global salary" and apply it uniformly. We apply a consistent methodology to each country's local market data. Internal equity is measured within country, not across countries. Cross-country salary comparisons are natural in a multi-country team and should be handled with a clear, prepared explanation of why differentiation is fair.
Country Profiles — Compensation Context

Handling Cross-Country Salary Questions
This is one of the most common and sensitive situations in multi-country HR. An employee discovers that a colleague in another country earns more (or less) and raises it as a fairness concern. Here is the prepared response framework.
The Prepared Explanation
"BaKuu Holding sets salaries based on the local market in each country. The cost of living, what other companies pay for the same role, and the local labour market are all different in Laos vs Indonesia vs Thailand. Our methodology is consistent — we benchmark every role against the same type of data in each country — but the resulting numbers are different. That is not unfair; it is economically accurate."
What You Should NOT Say
  • ✗"That is confidential, I cannot discuss it." — This confirms a problem exists without explaining it.
  • ✗"Everyone is paid fairly." — Vague. Creates more suspicion, not less.
  • ✗Sharing another employee's specific salary. — Never.

The Golden Rules of C&B Governance
!
No verbal salary commitments — everAny salary commitment made verbally by a manager, COO, or CEO without HR involvement and written confirmation is not binding on the company — but it will damage trust when it is not honoured. All salary discussions must be followed by written confirmation signed by HR.
!
No salary information sharing between employeesManagers must not share specific salary figures of other employees. HR must not share salary data except through official total rewards statements. Payslips are personal and confidential.
★
HR is the custodian of all salary dataAll salary records, offer letters, merit increase confirmations, and bonus letters are stored in the HR file. HR prepares the annual compensation report for CEO and COO. No other function holds master salary data.
★
Exceptions create precedent — document carefullyEvery approved exception to this framework must be documented with a clear rationale. Undocumented exceptions become informal policies that are impossible to enforce or defend later.
Chapter VII

Employee
Communication

A great total rewards package that employees do not understand is a wasted investment. This chapter covers how to communicate compensation clearly, when to say what, and how to handle sensitive conversations.

The Annual Compensation Communication Calendar
📋
January — Merit Increase Letters
Individual letters to all employees confirming their 2027 salary effective date, new amount, and the PM rating that drove the decision. Delivered by manager, countersigned by employee. HR retains original.
📊
February — Total Rewards Statements
Annual statement showing each employee their full package value: base salary, bonus received, benefits value, allowances, and development investment. Generated from Tool 7. Many employees underestimate their total package by 20–30%.
💬
Q1 — Bonus Payment & Communication
Annual bonus paid with March payroll. Individual bonus letters issued confirming the calculation: company modifier × individual PM rating × target percentage × base salary. No surprises.
🔄
Q4 — Market Positioning Review (Internal)
HR reviews salary bands against market benchmarks. Any adjustments to bands take effect from January. Employees are not individually notified of band adjustments — only of changes to their own salary.
📅
As Needed — New Hire Offers
Offers presented as total package — not just base salary. Include salary, allowances, benefits summary, and development offer. HR prepares offer letter. Manager delivers with verbal context.
Handling the "Why Did I Only Get X% Increase?" Conversation
This is the most common and most important compensation conversation managers face. Having a clear, confident answer is essential — vague responses damage trust more than the number itself.
Prepared Response Framework
"Your increase this year is [X]%. That is driven by two things: your performance rating of [N], which puts you in the [rating label] category, and your current position in the salary band, which is at [compa-ratio]%. The merit matrix for your rating and band position produces an increase in the [X–Y]% range, and your [X]% sits within that. If you want to understand what would drive a higher increase next year, the answer is [specific development goal or performance target]. I am happy to talk through that."
The key is specificityGeneric answers ("we consider many factors") create more suspicion than a specific answer that the employee disagrees with. A specific, logical answer — even one they do not like — is better than a vague one every time.

Scope
All employees of BaKuu Holding Group and its operating entities in Laos, Myanmar, Indonesia, and Thailand. Applicable to all employment types including local national and expatriate staff.
Core Philosophy Statement
BaKuu Holding rewards people for the value of their role as determined by market data, and recognises individual performance through merit increases, variable pay, and development investment. We are committed to internal equity, market competitiveness, and transparent communication of our rewards approach.
Market Positioning Policy
Critical roles (Site Manager, Drilling Supervisor): P65–75. High criticality roles (Group HR Manager, Finance Manager, HSE Officer): P55–65. Standard roles: P50. Entry level: P40–50. Market data reviewed annually using mining and oil & gas sector benchmarks.
Performance-Reward Link
Annual merit increases are determined by PM rating (from the BaKuu Holding PM System) combined with compa-ratio position. The merit matrix (Tool 4) produces increase ranges per rating and band position. Bonus eligibility requires Rating 3 or above.
Governance
HR owns the Total Rewards framework and is the custodian of all compensation data. Annual merit increases are approved by COO. New hires above band midpoint, off-cycle increases, retention bonuses, and all exceptions require CEO approval.
Country Differentiation
Salaries are benchmarked in local currency against local market data in each of the four operating countries. Internal equity is assessed within each country. Cross-country comparisons are not a basis for salary adjustment.
