PM Tool 3 — Performance Improvement Plan

Overview & When to Use a PIP

A PIP is a formal legal document — not an HR conversation. It exists to give an employee a structured, documented opportunity to improve before a termination decision. Use it correctly and it protects both parties. Misuse it as a paper trail to justify a decision already made, and it becomes a liability.

Critical — Read Before Initiating Any PIP A PIP must never be the first time an employee hears about a performance problem. Prior informal feedback and documented mid-year or annual review concerns must exist before a PIP is appropriate. A PIP initiated without prior documentation is legally weak and ethically indefensible.
What a PIP Is — and Is Not
A PIP IS
  • ✓ A genuine opportunity for the employee to improve
  • ✓ A formal, dated, signed document
  • ✓ A structured plan with clear, measurable goals
  • ✓ A document that protects the company if termination follows
  • ✓ A record that support was offered and the employee was aware
A PIP IS NOT
  • ✗ A decision to terminate with paperwork to justify it
  • ✗ A substitute for ongoing management and feedback
  • ✗ A disciplinary action (that is a separate process)
  • ✗ Appropriate for misconduct (use disciplinary procedure instead)
  • ✗ The first step — informal coaching must come first
Decision Tree — Should You Initiate a PIP?
Work through these questions before initiating. If you answer No to any of them, do not initiate a PIP yet — take the indicated action first.
Question 1
Has the employee been given informal feedback on this issue?
Verbal feedback in 1:1s, coaching conversations, or informal check-ins. If No → Start there. Have the conversation first. Document it with a follow-up email.
Question 2
Is the performance concern documented in a review (mid-year or annual)?
Rating 2 at annual review with documented concern, or written mid-year concern on file. If No → Issue a formal written warning first, confirm HR has a copy.
Question 3
Has the employee had a reasonable opportunity to improve after feedback?
At least 30–60 days since formal feedback was given and performance remains below standard. If No → Allow the improvement period first before escalating to PIP.
Question 4
Is the performance issue attributable to the employee — not to the role, tools, or management?
Exclude structural factors: inadequate tools, unclear instructions, unrealistic targets, or management failure. If No → Fix the root cause. A PIP will not fix a management or resourcing problem.
Question 5
Has HR reviewed and approved the decision to initiate a PIP?
If Yes to all of the above → Proceed. Use the Initiate a PIP section. HR must review and sign off before the document is issued to the employee.
Standard PIP Timeline
BaKuu Holding standard PIP is 30–90 days depending on role complexity. Site roles typically use 30 days. Office roles typically use 60–90 days. Indonesia requires adherence to SP1/SP2/SP3 letter structure (see Legal tab).

Step 1 — HR Must Be Involved Before Proceeding

Initiate a PIP

Complete all fields before issuing the PIP document to the employee. The PIP meeting must be attended by the manager, the employee, and HR (or an HR representative). The document is not valid without all three signatures.

Before You Start Confirm with HR that prior documentation exists. Have ready: the most recent review form showing the Rating 2, any written warnings on file, and specific examples of the performance gaps with dates.
Employee & PIP Details
Performance Concerns — Documented Gaps
State the specific performance concerns clearly and objectively. Use observed behaviour and measurable outcomes — not opinions or personality judgements. Each concern must be linked to an agreed goal or performance standard.
Performance Gap 1
Improvement Requirements
Define exactly what improvement is required to close the PIP successfully. Requirements must be specific, measurable, and achievable within the PIP period. Vague requirements ("improve attitude") are not acceptable and will not hold up legally.
Requirement 1
Support Offered by the Company
A legally sound PIP documents what support the company will provide. This demonstrates good faith and prevents a claim that the employee was set up to fail.
Consequences If PIP Is Not Met
This section must be stated clearly. The employee must understand what will happen if the PIP is not successfully completed. Do not be vague — this is what protects the company if termination follows.
Ongoing — Updated at Each Check-In

Milestone Tracker

Track progress at each formal check-in point. Every milestone review must be documented and signed by the employee and manager. Do not rely on memory — use this tracker to maintain a contemporaneous record.

Why Documentation at Every Milestone Matters If the PIP outcome leads to termination and the employee disputes it, the company must demonstrate that: (1) the employee knew the requirements, (2) progress was reviewed regularly, (3) the employee had genuine opportunities to improve, and (4) the final decision was based on documented evidence — not opinion. End of PIP Period — Three Possible Outcomes

Outcome & Closure

At the end of the PIP period, there are three possible outcomes. Each outcome has a distinct process and documentation requirement. HR must be involved in all outcome decisions — particularly for Outcome 3.

Outcome 1 — Employee Has Met All PIP Requirements The employee has demonstrated sustained improvement and met all requirements set out in the PIP. The PIP is closed. This is a positive outcome and should be communicated as such.
PIP Closure — Passed
Document the closure formally. The employee should receive written confirmation that the PIP has been closed and that they have met the required standard. This matters — do not close a PIP silently.
Standard Closure Letter Language

Dear [Employee Name],

We are writing to confirm that your Performance Improvement Plan, which commenced on [Start Date] and concluded on [End Date], has been successfully completed.

You have demonstrated sustained improvement across the requirements set out in the PIP and have met the required performance standard. We are pleased to confirm that the PIP is now formally closed with a satisfactory outcome.

We recognise the effort you have made during this period and encourage you to maintain this standard going forward. Your performance will continue to be reviewed as part of BaKuu Holding Group's standard annual review process.

Please sign and return the copy of this letter to confirm receipt.

Yours sincerely,
[Manager Name] / [HR Representative]
BaKuu Holding Group

Outcome 2 — Employee Is Making Progress But Has Not Yet Met All Requirements The employee has shown genuine effort and some improvement, but has not yet fully met the PIP requirements by the end date. An extension may be appropriate — but only once, and only with a specific, documented rationale.
PIP Extension — Guidelines
An extension is not automatic. It requires a deliberate decision by HR and the manager together. Use the checklist below before approving an extension.
Outcome 3 — Employee Has Not Met PIP Requirements The employee has failed to meet the requirements despite the PIP period and documented support. The company may now proceed to termination. This outcome requires legal review before any action is taken.

Legal Reference — By Country of Operation

Legal Language & Country Rules

BaKuu Holding operates across four countries with different labour law requirements. The process that is sufficient in Laos may be wholly inadequate in Indonesia. This section contains the minimum legal requirements and safe language templates for each country.

Disclaimer This guide is an HR reference tool based on publicly available labour law as at 2025. It is not legal advice. For any termination decision, BaKuu Holding should engage local legal counsel. Labour law in Laos and Myanmar in particular is subject to change and inconsistent enforcement.
Laos — Labour Law 2014 (as amended)
Minimum Process Before Termination for Performance
  1. 1 Verbal warning — documented in writing with a follow-up note to employee
  2. 2 First Written Warning (issued, signed, filed)
  3. 3 PIP issued with clear requirements and timeline
  4. 4 Termination notice (minimum notice per contract, typically 30 days) + severance per law
Notice Period
Minimum 30 days written notice, or payment in lieu of notice
Severance
Based on years of service: 10 days salary per year of service (minimum). Check employment contract for any enhancement.
Laos — Safe Language for Written Warning
WRITTEN WARNING — PERFORMANCE (LAOS)

TO: [Employee Full Name] | DATE: [Date] | REF: [Reference Number]

This letter constitutes a formal written warning regarding your performance in the role of [Job Title] at BaKuu Holding Group, [Location], Laos.

On [date(s)], your manager [Manager Name] discussed with you the following performance concerns: [describe specific performance gaps with measurable evidence and dates].

Despite prior verbal feedback given on [date(s)], the required improvement has not been achieved. The required standard is [state specific standard]. Your current performance is [state actual performance with data].

This written warning is formal notice that failure to achieve the required standard within [X days / by date] may result in further disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment in accordance with BaKuu Holding Group HR policy and applicable Lao labour law.

You are entitled to respond to this warning in writing within 5 working days. Your response will be attached to this document and filed in your HR record.

Myanmar — Employment and Skill Development Law 2013
HR Note on Myanmar Operations The political and regulatory environment in Myanmar post-2021 creates significant uncertainty for HR processes. All performance documentation for Myanmar employees should be maintained in both Myanmar and in the Singapore or Laos entity records. Engage local legal counsel before any termination decision.
Minimum Process Before Termination for Performance
  1. 1 Documented informal feedback and coaching — minimum 30 days
  2. 2 Written warning issued and signed — meticulous record-keeping required
  3. 3 PIP issued — copies held by both BaKuu Holding Myanmar entity and Singapore/Laos records
  4. 4 Legal counsel engaged. Termination notice and severance per contract and applicable law.
Notice Period
Per employment contract. Minimum 30 days for permanent employees. Payment in lieu acceptable.
Key Risk
Regulatory environment unpredictable. Always backup records outside Myanmar. Engage local counsel for any individual termination.
Indonesia — Manpower Law No. 13 of 2003
Indonesia Has the Strongest Employee Protections in the Region The SP1/SP2/SP3 letter structure is mandatory before performance termination. Skipping any letter or issuing them without proper intervals creates a void in the process. Courts have ordered reinstatement and full back-pay in cases where this process was not followed.
Mandatory SP Letter Sequence
SP1
Surat Peringatan 1 — First Warning Letter
Validity: 6 months from issue date. States the specific performance concern, required improvement, and consequence of non-improvement. Must be signed by employee.
SP2
Surat Peringatan 2 — Second Warning Letter
Issued if performance has not improved after SP1 (must still be within the 6-month SP1 validity window). States that performance has not improved despite SP1. Validity: 6 months from SP2 issue date.
SP3
Surat Peringatan 3 — Third Warning Letter + Termination Notice
Issued only after SP1 and SP2 have been issued and performance remains below standard. SP3 constitutes the final warning and signals that termination proceedings will follow if no improvement. After SP3, termination may be initiated.
Severance (Uang Pesangon)
Mandatory. Calculation based on years of service, seniority allowance, and service-rights compensation. Minimum 1x severance for performance termination. Consult payroll for exact calculation.
Notice Period
30 days written notice minimum. For site (PKWT) workers, contract end terms may differ. Review employment contract type before proceeding.
Thailand — Labour Protection Act B.E. 2541 (1998, as amended)
Minimum Process Before Termination for Performance
  1. 1 Written warning — issued, signed, filed. (Not legally required for gross misconduct, but required for performance.)
  2. 2 PIP with documented requirements, support provided, and milestones reviewed
  3. 3 Termination with notice (minimum 1 wage period) or payment in lieu. Severance mandatory for employees with 120+ days service.
Severance (Statutory)
30 days salary (under 1yr) → 90 days (1–3yr) → 180 days (3–6yr) → 240 days (6–10yr) → 300 days (10–20yr) → 400 days (20yr+). Performance termination = full severance unless gross misconduct proven.
Probationary Employees
Easier to terminate during probation (typically 119 days — keep under 120 to avoid full severance threshold). Document reason even during probation.
