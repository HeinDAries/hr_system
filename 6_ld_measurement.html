L&D Tool 6 / 7 — L&D Measurement & ROI

Why Measurement
Matters

L&D measurement at BaKuu Holding is not about proving that training happened. It is about proving that training changed something — conversion rates, programme delivery, retention, pipeline depth. If a learning investment cannot be connected to one of these outcomes, it is not a priority investment.

The Measurement Imperative at BaKuu Holding
People cost is 43% of revenue. Ba is not yet in business planning. These two facts are connected.
When L&D is invisible — when Ba cannot answer "what did the G4 coaching programme cost and what did it return?" — HR is a cost centre. When Ba can answer that question in revenue language — "the coaching programme moved Myanmar's conversion rate from 58% to 68%, saving $22K in replacement cost over 12 months against a $12K programme spend" — HR is an investment. The measurement system in this tool is the mechanism for making that shift from invisible to visible, from cost to investment.
The Three Measurement Purposes at BaKuu Holding
PURPOSE 1 — IMPROVE
Make the next L&D investment better
Measurement tells Ba which parts of a learning programme are working and which are not. The G4 coaching programme's six-module rubric score distribution tells Ba which module is weakest — and whether the weakness is the content, the facilitator, or the G4's context. Without measurement, Ba has opinions. With it, Ba has decisions.
PURPOSE 2 — JUSTIFY
Build the business case for future investment
The COO will approve a second year of the coaching programme faster if Ba can show what the first year returned. The data needed to justify next year's L&D budget is generated by measuring this year's outcomes. Measurement today is investment authority tomorrow.
PURPOSE 3 — POSITION
Build Ba's seat at the business planning table
Ba is not currently in business planning meetings. The fastest route into that room is to deliver intelligence that the COO cannot get anywhere else — pipeline risk in revenue terms, L&D ROI in margin language, workforce cost modelled against growth scenarios. This tool builds that intelligence system.
What BaKuu Holding Can Actually Measure
A 42-person company across four countries cannot run a multi-year randomised control trial. Measurement must be practical, low-overhead, and directly connected to data that already exists or can be collected without significant new infrastructure.

Measurement Traps — What NOT to Measure at BaKuu Holding
The most common measurement mistake in L&D is measuring what is easy to count rather than what the business cares about. These are the traps to avoid.
Metric to avoidWhy it is misleadingWhat to measure instead
Training hours deliveredA G4 who attended 30 hours of coaching training but still has a 50% conversion rate has not learned what matters. Hours delivered measures activity, not change.G4 coaching rubric score (before/after). Conversion rate change 6 months after programme completion.

Section 02 — Measurement Framework

Kirkpatrick–Phillips
at BaKuu Holding

The Kirkpatrick–Phillips five-level model is the reference framework for L&D measurement globally. At BaKuu Holding, not every level is applicable to every programme — and that is by design. This section translates each level into BaKuu Holding language and identifies which programmes warrant measurement at which levels.

"Don't measure every level for every programme. Measure the levels that answer the questions the business is actually asking about this investment."

BaKuu Holding L&D Measurement Principle · 2026
1
Reaction
What it measures
Did participants find the learning experience relevant, engaging, and useful? Immediate post-session response.
BaKuu Holding application
Minimal use. Only applied to the G4 coaching programme and SM peer forum — and only in the first delivery cycle to check design quality. Three questions maximum: relevant? practical? one change I will make?
BaKuu Holding programmes
G4 coaching — first cycle only SM peer forum — first 2 sessions
2
Learning
What it measures
Did participants acquire the knowledge, skills, or attitude the programme was designed to develop? Assessed during or immediately after the programme.
BaKuu Holding application
Applied to the G4 coaching programme through the five-dimension rubric — scored before and after the six-module programme. The rubric score increase is the Level 2 measure. Also applied to G1–G3 milestone sign-off — milestone completion is evidence of learning demonstrated.
BaKuu Holding programmes
G4 coaching — rubric score G1–G4 milestones — completion
3
Behaviour
What it measures
Did participants change their on-the-job behaviour as a result of the programme? Observed 30–90 days after programme completion.
BaKuu Holding application
The highest-priority level at BaKuu Holding. G4 coaching programme: is the G4 running structured debriefs, documenting month-3 check-ins, and flagging at-risk trainees? Observed by SM. SM peer forum: is the SM applying the commitment they made at the forum? Ba checks at the next forum.
BaKuu Holding programmes
G4 coaching — debrief frequency SM peer forum — commitment follow-up G1–G4 pathway — next-grade behaviour
4
Results
What it measures
Did the programme produce measurable business outcomes — productivity, quality, retention, delivery? Measured 3–12 months after programme completion.
BaKuu Holding application
The primary measure for Ba's COO reporting. G4 coaching programme: conversion rate change at 6 and 12 months post-certification. Laos G3 development plan: first G4 certification date vs. first retirement date. Indonesia sourcing + pipeline programme: G3 headcount and delivery % recovery.
BaKuu Holding programmes
G4 coaching — conversion rate Laos G3 plans — certification date Indonesia pipeline — delivery %
5
ROI
What it measures
Did the financial benefit of the programme's results exceed its cost? Expressed as a percentage return: (Benefit − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100.
BaKuu Holding application
Applied to the G4 coaching programme and the Laos G3 development investment — the two programmes with the largest quantifiable business stakes. The ROI Calculator (Section 07) runs the calculation. Not required for every programme — only those where the COO needs to see the investment case in financial terms.

Section 04 — ROI: The Driller Pipeline

ROI: The Driller
Pipeline

The two L&D investments with the largest quantifiable return at BaKuu Holding are the G4 coaching programme and the Laos G3 development plan. This section builds the full financial case for each — costs, benefits, and ROI in terms the COO cannot ignore.

The ROI Argument — Framing for the COO
Every trained driller who exits unnecessarily costs $1,000–$2,000 — before production downtime.
At 58% conversion with a cohort of 8 trainees, Myanmar produces approximately 4–5 G2 drillers. At 70% it produces 5–6. The difference is 1 additional driller per cohort. With 2 cohorts per year, that is 2 additional G2 drillers annually. At a replacement cost of $1,000–$2,000 per driller (recruitment, onboarding, lost productivity during gap) — plus the production downtime cost that Ba does not yet have a figure for — the benefit side of the coaching programme ROI is real, computable, and substantial relative to the programme cost. This is Ba's number to own.

Section 05 — Data Collection System

Data Collection
System

A measurement system is only as good as its data. This section defines exactly what Ba collects, from whom, when, and how — using a deliberately light-touch approach that does not require new technology or significant overhead from the field.

Design principle: pull from what already exists first.Operations reports, payroll records, and SM monthly updates contain most of what Ba needs. The measurement system adds only what cannot be extracted from existing sources — primarily the G4 coaching observation record and the milestone completion log. Avoid creating new data-collection burden that SMs and G4s will not sustain.
