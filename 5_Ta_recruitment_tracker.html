Counter-offer handling · step-by-step protocol
⇄
When a Candidate Counter-Offers
What to do immediately and what to avoid
01
Acknowledge, don't react
Say: "Thank you for sharing that — let me take this back and get back to you by [specific date, max 2 business days]." Never negotiate live in the first call. You need time to consult the Hiring Manager and COO on the upper limit.
02
Understand the real objection
Before going back, call the candidate back informally: "Beyond salary, is there anything else about the offer or the role that you'd want us to address?" Sometimes counter-offers are about non-monetary terms (rotation schedule, R&R, title) — which are cheaper to fix.
03
Know your BATNA before negotiating
What is the maximum you can offer? Is there flexibility on non-cash terms (extra flights, housing allowance, start date)? What happens if this candidate says no — do you have a backup? If no backup, your negotiating position is weak. Be honest about that internally.
04
Make one counter — not an auction
Come back with your best and final offer. Say: "We've reviewed and this is our best offer: [X]. We're not able to go further on base but we can offer [Y non-cash term]." Multiple small increments invite repeated counters. One clear final offer forces a decision.
05
Give a deadline — and enforce it
"We need your decision by [specific date] as we have other candidates progressing." This is real — you should have another candidate in reserve for all critical roles. Without a deadline, candidates stall indefinitely while shopping competing offers.
06
If they decline — process cleanly
Thank them, document in the tracker, and return to your pipeline. Do not burn the relationship — they may come back in 6–12 months, or refer someone. The SEA drilling community is small. How you handle rejection is part of your employer brand.
⚑
When NOT to Counter-Counter
Know when to walk away
Counter exceeds salary band by >20%If accepting would create internal equity issues or set an unsustainable precedent, decline. Compromising compensation structure for one hire costs more over time.
Third round of negotiationIf you're on the third exchange, the candidate is either genuinely misaligned or using your offer to lever another company. Both are bad. Make one final offer and mean it.
Candidate has missed their own deadlineIf they asked for time and haven't responded by their own stated date, chase once. If still no response, withdraw the offer professionally. This behaviour predicts unreliability on site.
Terms requested are non-negotiable (location, rotation)If a candidate's counter involves changing fundamental role parameters — they're not the right fit. Better to know now than after mobilisation.

This was a competitive process and the decision was not easy. We would like to stay in touch as our operations expand across the region, and we hope you will keep us in mind for future roles as well. If you would find it helpful, I am happy to share brief feedback from the interview. Please let me know if that would be useful. We wish you every success. [Your Name] Group HR Manager, Valentis Laos
WhatsApp — Quick Rejection (Post-Screen)
Hi [Name], thank you again for your time. After reviewing all applications for the [Role] position, we've decided to move forward with other candidates at this stage. We appreciate your interest and will keep your details on file for future opportunities. Best wishes.
Never ghost a candidate — even a short rejection message is better than silence. In the SEA drilling community, word travels fast. How you reject people is part of your employer brand and affects future referrals and applications.
