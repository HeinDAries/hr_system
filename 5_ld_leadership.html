L&D Tool 5 / 7 — Leadership Development Programme

Leadership at
BaKuu Holding

Leadership development at BaKuu Holding is not a training calendar. It is a deliberate system for building the people accountability that keeps operations running, pipelines full, and the business capable of growth. Every leadership development investment at BaKuu Holding is justified by one question: does this make the organisation more resilient or more capable of execution?

The Leadership Reality at BaKuu Holding
Four Site Managers, four countries, four different leadership philosophies. Only one is producing the results BaKuu Holding needs.
Thailand SM operates at 105% country factor with the highest G1 conversion rate in the group. Myanmar SM has three G4 supervisors with a coaching quality gap driving 58% conversion. Indonesia SM is resistant to Group HR engagement and managing a three-vacancy G3 crisis. Laos SM has a retirement cliff forming beneath their feet and two G3 candidates who may or may not be G4-ready in time. Leadership development at BaKuu Holding is not abstract — it is the direct lever on every people risk in the business.

Section 02 — Leadership Framework

The BaKuu Holding
Leadership Framework

Four capabilities. Not twenty. The BaKuu Holding Leadership Framework is built for a drilling operation leader who works in a remote, multilingual, high-stakes environment with limited support infrastructure. Every capability is observable, measurable, and directly connected to a business outcome.

"At BaKuu Holding, leadership is not about style. It is about whether the people under your leadership develop, perform, and stay — and whether the operation delivers what it promised."

BaKuu Holding Leadership Framework · 2026
C1
Develop People
Pipeline accountability
WHAT IT MEANS
Creates deliberate development experiences for the people they lead. Runs structured debrief conversations. Monitors at-risk signals. Gives developmental feedback. Is accountable for the conversion rate of people in their care — not just whether those people are assigned work.
OBSERVABLE INDICATOR
G4: conversion rate ≥70%, check-ins documented. SM: G4 coaching quality improving, G3 pipeline candidates identified and developing. Ba: SM development plans active, pipeline data in COO briefings.
FAILURE MODE
Assigns work but does not develop. Sees people development as HR's job, not their job. Avoids difficult conversations. Conversion rate stagnates and attrition is attributed to external factors.
C2
Deliver Results
Operational accountability
WHAT IT MEANS
Plans and executes operational work to target. Manages resources — people, equipment, time — to deliver programme commitments. Identifies and responds to deviations. Does not just describe problems; presents the response or asks for what they need to respond.
OBSERVABLE INDICATOR
G4: programme delivery ≥93%, shift records complete and accurate. SM: country delivery ≥93%, variance reports include response plan, client commitments met. Ba: workforce planning data used in business planning conversations.
FAILURE MODE
Reactive rather than planned. Reports problems without proposals. Accepts underperformance as external. Delivery data is late or incomplete — the SM who does not track is the SM who cannot improve.
C3
Build Trust
Relational accountability
WHAT IT MEANS
Creates the conditions in which people are willing to share what is really happening — risks, concerns, difficulties — before they become incidents or departures. Follows through on commitments. Is consistent in behaviour regardless of who is watching. Does what they said they would do.
OBSERVABLE INDICATOR
G4: trainees raise concerns before they escalate; G4 commitments at end of debrief sessions are followed up. SM: G4s flag at-risk situations proactively; attrition does not include "could not talk to SM" as exit reason.
FAILURE MODE
People do not surface problems until they are crises. Commitments are made in conversations and forgotten. The G4 who says "I'll look into that" and never does loses the trainee's trust — and the trainee exits without warning at month 3.
C4
Think Ahead
Strategic accountability
WHAT IT MEANS
Anticipates risks and opportunities before they become urgent. Plans beyond the current shift or current month. Connects site-level decisions to group-level consequences. Raises concerns with enough lead time for Group HR or COO to act. Does not wait to be asked.
OBSERVABLE INDICATOR
SM: raises succession risks before they become gaps; proposes pipeline solutions at quarterly talent review; initiates conversation with Ba before waiting for directive. G4: flags at-risk trainee before check-in triggers it.
FAILURE MODE
Reactive to every situation. No succession plan until a retirement is imminent. Does not raise the Laos retirement risk until a G4 hands in their notice. The SM who waits for Group HR to ask is not demonstrating C4.

The Quarterly G4 Development Debrief
Ba conducts a 30-minute development debrief with each G4 every quarter — immediately after the quarterly pipeline report is submitted. The debrief uses conversion rate data, check-in completion data, and G4's own reflections to generate one development focus for the next quarter.
Q1 — DATA REVIEW
Ba reviews conversion rate, check-in completion, and milestone sign-off quality for the prior quarter. Data is shared with the G4 before the conversation — no surprises. "Here's what the data shows — what do you make of it?"
Q2 — REFLECTION
"What has been hardest in your coaching this quarter? What have you noticed about yourself as a developer?" Ba holds silence. The four-question debrief format is used — Ba models the coaching behaviour they expect the G4 to use with trainees.
Q3 — ONE FOCUS
"What is the one thing you will focus on in your coaching this quarter that would make the biggest difference to [trainee name]'s development?" Specific, named, time-bound. Ba documents and opens next quarter's debrief by reviewing it.

Criterion 1 — Country-level delivery ownership. Has managed a full programme cycle — planning through client reporting — without SM supervision. The G4 who has run a programme segment (G3-M2 milestone quality) is the starting point.
Criterion 2 — Multi-person leadership. Has led a team of 4+ people with different grades and experience levels. Not just supervised — developed. Conversion rate and milestone quality are the evidence.
Criterion 3 — External relationship management. Has represented BaKuu Holding in a client-facing or stakeholder context — programme update, site visit, or safety review. Able to present operational performance clearly to a non-technical audience.
Criterion 4 — Multi-country or multi-site awareness. Has operated in a context different from their home country (cross-site exposure) and can connect local decisions to group-level consequences. C4 (Think Ahead) at group scope, not just country scope.
Criterion 5 — Ba endorsement. Group HR has assessed the candidate using the C1–C4 framework and confirmed readiness. Ba brings the pipeline intelligence — the SM nomination + Ba endorsement go to COO together.

Section 06 — Country 5 Leadership Readiness

Country 5
Leadership Readiness

Country 5 launches Q2 2027. The SM candidate must be identified by Q3 2026. The G4 candidates must be G4-certified before deployment. This section defines what "Country 5 ready" looks like for leadership, and what Ba must build between now and Q2 2027 to make that readiness real.

The Country 5 leadership readiness deadline is Q3 2026 — not Q2 2027. Identifying and developing the Country 5 leadership team takes 6–9 months minimum. If the SM candidate is not identified by Q3 2026 and their development plan activated, the Q2 2027 launch will either be delayed or staffed with an underprepared leader. Ba must drive the identification process — it will not happen without Group HR initiating it.
Country 5 SM — What "Ready" Looks Like
The Country 5 SM will be setting up a new BaKuu Holding operation from scratch — without the established team, site infrastructure, and operational norms that exist in Laos, Myanmar, Indonesia, or Thailand. The readiness criteria are higher than for an existing country SM role.
C1 — Develop People at setup speed. Country 5 will need to recruit, onboard, and develop a full G1 cohort from day one. The SM must be able to run an induction programme and G4 coaching framework without Group HR on site. Thailand SM's documented practices are the reference resource.
C2 — Deliver from day one with an inexperienced team. Programme targets in Country 5 will need to be met while the team is still in the first 90 days. The SM must manage programme delivery expectations with the client while managing a development-heavy workforce. This requires unusual skill at managing upward and outward simultaneously.
C3 — Build trust without history. The Country 5 SM arrives unknown. They must build trust with a new team, a new client, and a new government regulatory context — simultaneously, from scratch. Their ability to be consistent and follow-through from day one is the foundation everything else is built on.
C4 — Think ahead at launch. The Country 5 SM must anticipate setup problems — workforce shortfalls, equipment delays, regulatory friction — and communicate them to the COO early. A Country 5 SM who surfaces problems only when they have already affected operations is not capable of the C4 standard the role requires.
Country 5 G4s — Deployment Criteria
Country 5 requires at least 2 G4-certified drillers from day one. These G4s must come from the existing internal pipeline — not external hire. External G4 hire with no BaKuu Holding context creates a coaching quality risk that undermines the pipeline from the start.
G4 Coaching Certification required. Both Country 5 G4s must have completed the G4 coaching programme and hold G4 Coaching Certification. They will be running G1 induction in Country 5 without Ba on site — the certification standard is the quality gate.
Cross-site experience preferred. G4 candidates for Country 5 should have cross-site exposure — either from their own development path or a specific placement. A G4 who has only operated in one country's conditions is less likely to adapt quickly to Country 5's unknown context.
Identification deadline Q4 2026. Country 5 G4 candidates must be identified by Q4 2026 to allow any development gap to be closed before Q2 2027 deployment. Ba reviews the group-wide G4 pipeline and proposes candidates to COO with SM endorsement.
