HR calibration guide · annual review · December
Annual review only. No employee receives a final rating until HR calibration is complete. This is non-negotiable.
◎
Why Calibration Exists
Different managers interpret the same performance level differently. Manager A's 3 may equal Manager B's 4. Without calibration, employees in different teams are judged by different standards — which is unfair and legally risky.
Calibration is not about hitting a rating quota. It is about ensuring the rating scale means the same thing across all BaKuu Holding managers, countries, and role types.
HR leads the calibration session. Managers attend but do not dominate. COO attends for senior roles or contested ratings.
◷
Calibration Process — Step by Step
1
HR collects all manager assessments (1 Dec)
All forms submitted to HR. HR reviews for completeness and flags missing evidence before the session.
2
HR builds distribution view (2–5 Dec)
HR plots all proposed ratings by team. Checks for leniency clustering, central tendency, or unsupported outliers.
3
Calibration session with managers (8–10 Dec)
HR-led. Managers with 5s or 1s present evidence. HR challenges inconsistencies. Agreed final ratings recorded.
4
Final ratings issued to employees (15–31 Dec)
Manager communicates rating face-to-face or by video call — never email alone. HR files all signed documents.
!
Common Rating Biases — What HR Looks For
These patterns trigger a calibration challenge
Bias TypeWhat It Looks LikeWhy It HappensHR Response
Leniency BiasManager rates everyone 4 or 5. No 3s or below in the team.Wanting to be liked. Avoiding difficult conversations. Guilt about salary constraints.Request evidence for all 4s and 5s. If thin, propose downgrade to 3.
Central TendencyManager rates everyone a 3 regardless of performance difference.Risk aversion. Fear of challenge. Unfamiliarity with the full scale.Ask manager to identify best and worst performer. If truly equal, document why.
Recency BiasRating reflects only the last 2–3 months, ignoring full year.Recent events are more vivid in memory.Request evidence from across the full 12-month period.
Halo EffectOne strong goal leads to high ratings on all other goals too.One impressive achievement colours the entire assessment.Review each goal independently. One excellent goal does not auto-elevate others.
Horns EffectOne mistake drags all ratings below what evidence supports.One failure is memorable. Manager anchors the whole assessment on it.Same as halo — each goal rated independently.
Affinity BiasEmployees similar to the manager consistently rated higher.Unconscious preference for people we identify with.Compare ratings across genders, nationalities, sites. Flag statistical outliers.

Manager guide · how to run a performance review conversation
This guide is for BaKuu Holding managers — particularly operations managers — who have not conducted structured performance reviews before. A review conversation is not an evaluation delivered to an employee. It is a two-way conversation. The manager speaks for 40% of the time. The employee speaks for 60%.
◷
Meeting Structure
60 min annual · 45 min mid-year
0–5 min
Open — Set the tone
Welcome, confirm agenda, create safety to speak honestly
5–20 min
Employee speaks first
Employee walks through self-assessment. Manager listens. Do not interrupt.
20–40 min
Goal-by-goal discussion
Manager shares assessment per goal. Evidence shared. Discussion where views differ.
40–55 min
Development and career conversation
Forward-looking. Aspirations. Development plan for next period.
55–60 min
Close and next steps
Summarise actions. Communicate rating (annual only). Confirm follow-up date.
✓
Before the Meeting — Manager Checklist
Step-by-step conversation guide · click to expand each section
1
Opening the Conversation
Set tone · confirm agenda · make the employee feel safe to speak honestly
5 min
►
What to say
"Thanks for taking the time. I want this to be a real conversation — not me reading scores to you. I have read your self-assessment and I have my own thoughts, but I want to hear from you first. Nothing here should surprise you — if it does, that is on me, not on you."
What not to say
✗ "This will not take long." — signals the conversation is an inconvenience
✗ "I have already decided your rating." — kills psychological safety immediately
✗ "HR told me I have to do this." — removes your ownership of the process
2
Letting the Employee Speak First
Employee reflects first · manager listens · do not correct until they finish
15 min
►
Useful opening questions
"Walk me through your self-assessment. What are you most proud of this period?"
"What felt hardest? What would you do differently?"
"Where do you feel you had less support than you needed?"
Active listening rule: after the employee answers, pause for 3 seconds before responding. Most managers respond too quickly and signal they were not really listening.
3
Giving Feedback — The SBI Model
Situation · Behaviour · Impact · the only safe way to give feedback
20 min
►
S
Situation
Name the specific situation. "In March, during the rig downtime…"
B
Behaviour
What they did or did not do — no judgement. "You escalated to the contractor within 2 hours and coordinated the repair schedule."
I
Impact
The result. "This kept us within 15% of weekly meterage target and avoided a client escalation."
Never use: "always", "never", "attitude", "lazy", or "difficult." These are judgements — they trigger defensiveness and in some countries create legal exposure.
4
Handling Disagreement or Emotional Reaction
When the employee disputes a rating or becomes upset
As needed
►
If the employee disagrees with a rating:Do not argue. Say: "I hear you — can you walk me through the evidence you have in mind?" Listen. If their evidence is strong, acknowledge it. If the rating should change, say you will discuss with HR before finalising. Never change a rating in the room under pressure.
If the employee becomes emotional:Pause. Do not keep talking. Say: "Take a moment — this conversation matters and I want us both to be present." Offer water. Allow time. Reschedule if necessary — do not push through a breakdown.
If a grievance is raised during the review:Acknowledge it, write it down, park it: "That is important — let me complete this conversation and I will follow up within 5 days." Do not try to resolve a formal grievance inside a performance review.
5
Development and Career Conversation
Forward-looking · aspirations · agree next period development plan
15 min
►
"Where do you see yourself in 2–3 years? What kind of role are you interested in?"
"What is the one skill or experience that would make the biggest difference to your career right now?"
"What can the company do better to support your development?"
After this section you should be able to name: (1) one concrete development action the employee will take in the next 6 months, and (2) one thing you will do to support it. If you cannot name both — the conversation was not complete.
6
Closing, Communicating the Rating, and Documenting
Summarise · communicate rating (annual) · confirm actions · sign forms
5 min
►
"To close — here is what I heard: [1–2 strengths, 1 development area]. Your development goal for next period is [goal]. I will [manager action] by [date]. For annual review: your confirmed rating is [X] — [rating name]. This reflects [brief rationale]. If you have questions, come to me or HR within 5 working days."
✓ Both parties sign the review form before the meeting ends
✓ Manager sends a one-paragraph summary email within 24 hours
✓ Signed review form submitted to HR within 3 working days of the meeting
