L&D Tool 1 / 7 — L&D Strategy & Framework

L&D Strategy
& Framework

L&D strategy is not a training calendar. It is the deliberate set of choices about what capability the organisation needs, where the gaps are, how learning will be delivered, and how the investment will be measured. At BaKuu Holding, L&D strategy is inseparable from the driller pipeline — because the pipeline is what the business runs on.

"BaKuu Holding does not have a training problem. It has a capability conversion problem. The question is not whether to invest in learning — it is whether the learning we are doing is actually converting G1 trainees into G4 senior drillers at the rate the business requires."

BaKuu Holding L&D Strategic Frame · 2026
What L&D Strategy Is — and Is Not
L&D Strategy IS
→ A deliberate choice about which capabilities to build and in what sequence
→ A framework for converting learning investment into business outcomes
→ A set of governance decisions about what is mandatory vs elective
→ A pipeline plan — linking learning to the G1→G4 development cycle
→ A measurement system that speaks in COO language
L&D Strategy IS NOT
— A list of courses available on request
— A training budget divided equally across countries
— A compliance tick-box (induction + annual safety training)
— Whatever the most vocal SM requested last quarter
— An activity report counting training hours delivered
The Three Strategic Questions
Q1 — What capability does the business need?
Not "what would people like to learn" — but "what capability does BaKuu Holding need to deliver its programmes at 93%+ across five countries by 2027?" This is the business-driven capability requirement that L&D strategy serves.
Q2 — Where are the gaps between current and required capability?
The Training Needs Analysis (L&D Tool 2) answers this question systematically. The gap is not uniform — Myanmar has a G4 coaching skills gap, Indonesia has a G3 technical gap from vacancies, Laos has a G3→G4 development gap with a 2027 retirement cliff.
Q3 — How will we close the gaps — and how will we know it worked?
The delivery model (70/20/10), the build vs buy decision, and the measurement framework. At BaKuu Holding, most learning happens on the job — the L&D strategy must be designed for that reality, not for a classroom-based corporate model.
The HRBP's Role in L&D Strategy
The HRBP does not deliver learning — SMs and G4 drillers do. The HRBP's role is to design the system, set the standards, measure the outcomes, and hold the pipeline accountable.

Section 02 — The 70/20/10 Model

The 70/20/10
Model

The 70/20/10 model describes how adults actually learn — and how learning investment should be allocated. It is not a rigid formula, but it is a powerful corrective to the default assumption that learning happens in classrooms and courses.

Why this model matters at BaKuu HoldingBaKuu Holding's workforce is field-based, technical, and distributed across four countries. Formal classroom training is expensive to deliver, difficult to schedule, and poorly retained by workers who learn by doing. The 70/20/10 model validates the approach BaKuu Holding's best G4 drillers already use intuitively — and gives the HRBP a framework to make it systematic rather than personality-dependent.

BaKuu Holding Learning Mix
70% — Learning Through Experience
The majority of capability development at every grade level happens through the work itself — not through training programmes. This is especially true for technical, field-based roles like drilling. A G1 trainee who spends 18 months working alongside a G4 driller on varied drill sites learns more than one who attends a 5-day technical course.
Stretch assignmentsDeliberately assigning trainees to tasks slightly above their current capability — with G4 support available. The stretch creates the learning. The support prevents failure.
Varied site exposureRotating trainees across different geological conditions, equipment configurations, and site challenges. At BaKuu Holding, cross-country deployment for G3 candidates serves this purpose — Laos G3 candidates gaining experience at Indonesia or Thailand sites accelerates development.
Milestone-based progressionStructuring the 18–24 month G1→G4 pathway as a sequence of observable milestones — not a time-based progression. The G1 advances to G2 when they can demonstrate the G2 capabilities, not when they have been employed for 6 months.

10% — Formal Learning
Formal learning — courses, workshops, certifications, structured programmes — represents only 10% of how learning actually occurs, but it tends to receive a disproportionate share of the L&D budget and attention. The risk at BaKuu Holding is defaulting to formal training as the response to every capability gap — because it is visible, schedulable, and easy to report.
Where formal learning adds most valueTechnical certifications (drilling-specific), safety qualifications (mandatory), regulatory compliance, leadership skills that are too abstract to learn purely on the job (coaching techniques, financial literacy for SMs), and HRBP professional development (AIHR certifications).
Where formal learning is over-usedAs a substitute for on-the-job practice. Sending a G3 driller on a 3-day leadership course does not make them a G4. Only sustained responsibility with support does that. Formal learning is most effective when it provides frameworks for experiences the learner is already having — not experiences they have not yet had.
Delivery realities at BaKuu HoldingField-based workforce across 4 countries means formal training must be practical to access — online where possible, modular for shift workers, and timed around operational cycles not administrative convenience. The Myanmar supervisor coaching programme (6 weeks, modular) is the right design for this context.

Section 03 — Build vs Buy vs Borrow

Build vs Buy
vs Borrow

Every L&D investment decision involves a sourcing choice — do we develop this capability internally (build), purchase it from an external provider (buy), or access it from a partner or peer network (borrow)? The wrong choice wastes money. The right choice depends on how strategically critical the capability is and how unique BaKuu Holding's requirements are.

Build
Develop capability internally — through structured on-the-job progression, internal coaching, and Group HR-designed programmes. The primary mode for operational and pipeline capability that is BaKuu Holding-specific.
Use when: capability is strategically critical AND highly BaKuu Holding-specific (e.g. G1→G4 technical progression, Myanmar site culture, BaKuu Holding programme methodology)
Buy
Purchase from external providers — certifications, technical training, compliance programmes, and leadership development content that is not BaKuu Holding-specific. Faster to deploy, consistent quality, no internal design burden.
Use when: capability requirement is standard across the industry AND quality external provision exists (e.g. drilling certifications, HSE programmes, AIHR certifications)
Borrow
Access capability through partnerships, peer networks, or cross-company learning arrangements. At BaKuu Holding scale, this means leveraging the SM peer network for practice transfer and using industry bodies for technical updates.
Use when: capability exists elsewhere in the organisation or industry AND formalising access is more efficient than building or buying (e.g. Thailand SM sharing practices, G3 cross-site exposure)
The Build vs Buy Decision Matrix
High Strategic Value + High BaKuu Holding-Specificity
→ BUILD
G1→G4 technical development pathway. G4 coaching programme (BaKuu Holding-specific content). Myanmar supervisor development (context-specific). Country 5 SM succession pipeline. These capabilities are too BaKuu Holding-specific to buy and too strategically important to leave to chance.
High Strategic Value + Low BaKuu Holding-Specificity
→ BUY (with internal application)
Technical drilling certifications — standards exist and external providers certify to a consistent standard. Leadership coaching models — buy the framework, build the BaKuu Holding application. HRBP professional development (AIHR) — buy the certification, apply to BaKuu Holding context. HSE compliance — buy the programme, meet the regulatory standard.
Low Strategic Value + High BaKuu Holding-Specificity
→ BORROW or DEFER
Onboarding content that is country-specific but not strategically critical — borrow from SM or HR Coordinator once in post. Administrative process training — borrow from Finance. Local labour law updates — borrow from local legal advisor. Do not design formal programmes for these.
Low Strategic Value + Low BaKuu Holding-Specificity
→ BUY cheap or DON'T
General computer skills, basic administrative training, generic communication skills. If genuinely needed, buy the cheapest adequate option. Do not invest Group HR design capacity here. At BaKuu Holding's current scale, this category should be nearly empty — every L&D dollar should be traceable to the pipeline.

Section 04 — L&D & the Driller Pipeline

L&D & the
Driller Pipeline

The driller pipeline is BaKuu Holding's most critical people risk. Every L&D investment decision should be evaluated against one primary question: does this accelerate or protect the G1→G4 conversion pipeline? If it does not, it is secondary.

The Business Case for Pipeline L&D
Conversion rate is a financial metric, not just an HR metric.
Myanmar's current G1→G4 conversion rate is 58% against a 70% target. At 8 trainees per cohort and $1,000–$2,000 replacement cost per lost trainee (excluding production downtime), each cohort running at 58% produces $1,600–$3,200 in avoidable replacement cost. Over three cohorts per year: $4,800–$9,600 direct cost per year — plus the pipeline compression that means BaKuu Holding reaches 2027 with fewer G4 drillers than the business plan requires. Every percentage point of conversion rate improvement is worth approximately $480–$960 per year in direct cost savings, before the pipeline value is counted. The Myanmar coaching programme (estimated cost: Group HR design time + 6 weeks SM involvement) will pay back within one cohort if it delivers a 7pp conversion rate improvement.

The Month-3 Check-in as an L&D Intervention
The month-3 check-in is the most important single L&D intervention at BaKuu Holding. Myanmar's attrition peak is at month 3 — the point at which trainees have enough experience to assess whether the job is what they expected, but have not yet developed the investment and identity that makes leaving costly.
What the check-in must doAssess whether the trainee's learning experience is meeting their expectations. Identify early capability gaps before they become failure. Surface any site-level issues (workload, team dynamics, perceived fairness) that are driving exit intent. Create a named relationship between the trainee and the G4 as the person who is investing in their development.
What makes it workThe G4 must be trained to conduct the conversation — not just told to have it. The conversation must be documented. The SM must see the summary. Group HR must be notified of any at-risk trainee within 48 hours of the check-in. The HRBP's job is to design the conversation guide, train the G4s to use it, and monitor completion across all countries.
Current state — MyanmarCheck-ins happen informally when the G4 thinks of it. No documentation. No notification to SM or Group HR. No consistent structure. The G4 who conducts a check-in well does so because they are a naturally strong developer — not because the system requires it. This is the gap the Myanmar coaching programme closes.
Section 05 — BaKuu Holding L&D Maturity

BaKuu Holding L&D
Maturity

L&D maturity describes the sophistication of an organisation's learning capability — from reactive and compliance-focused at Level 1, to strategically integrated and pipeline-driving at Level 5. Knowing where BaKuu Holding sits today is the basis for knowing what to build next.

The Five L&D Maturity Levels
Level 1
Reactive
L&D is compliance-driven — safety training, induction, mandatory certifications. Training happens in response to incidents or regulatory requirements. No TNA process. No pipeline focus. L&D budget is a compliance cost, not an investment. No Group HR ownership of the curriculum.
Level 2
Structured
Current — BaKuu Holding
Some structured development pathways exist. Induction is formalised. Some technical training is in place. Training requests are managed (if informally). The G1→G4 pathway is partially defined but not consistently applied across countries. Performance development conversations happen but are not linked to a formal capability framework. L&D is owned by Group HR in principle but executed inconsistently in practice.
Level 3
Strategic
Target — 2026
L&D is explicitly linked to business strategy and the driller pipeline. TNA drives the curriculum rather than ad hoc requests. The G1→G4 pathway is fully defined, consistently applied, and milestone-based across all countries. G4 coaching capability is designed and trained. Conversion rate is the primary L&D metric. Group HR reports on pipeline outcomes quarterly to the COO. Build vs buy decisions are made deliberately.
Level 4
Integrated
Target — 2027 / Country 5 entry
L&D is embedded in workforce planning, succession, and performance management — not a separate function. Development plans are standard at all grades. Succession readiness is assessed and managed. The SM peer network is a designed learning mechanism. Cross-country practice transfer is systematic. Country 5 onboarding uses the fully developed pathway and requires no Group HR reinvention.
Level 5
Learning Organisation
Horizon — 2028+
Continuous learning is embedded in how BaKuu Holding operates. After-action reviews are standard. Innovation is captured and shared. The organisation learns from near-misses, not just successes. L&D is measured on business outcomes — pipeline fill rate, programme delivery %, revenue per G4. The CHRO is a strategic partner to the CEO on talent as a competitive advantage.
Level 2 → Level 3 Gap Analysis
The shift from Level 2 (Structured) to Level 3 (Strategic) is the primary L&D transformation task for Ba in 2026. The gap is not about adding more training — it is about making the existing learning more deliberate, consistent, and measurable.

Section 06 — L&D Design Principles

L&D Design
Principles

Design principles are the commitments that guide every L&D decision at BaKuu Holding. They are the filter applied before any programme is designed, any training budget is approved, or any intervention is proposed. Principles without decisions are aspirational. Decisions without principles are inconsistent.

P1
Pipeline first — every L&D decision is evaluated against its impact on the G1→G4 conversion rate
Before any L&D investment is approved, the first question is: does this contribute to converting G1 trainees into G4 senior drillers? If yes — proceed. If no — what is the justification for doing it ahead of the pipeline work? This principle does not prevent investment in leadership development, SM capability, or HRBP professional growth — but it requires those investments to be positioned correctly in the priority queue.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: Myanmar coaching programme approved before SM financial literacy — because it directly addresses the 58% conversion rate. SM financial literacy is secondary, approved as a secondary priority.
P2
Design for the field — not the classroom
BaKuu Holding's workforce is field-based, technically skilled, often operating in remote locations across four countries with different languages and cultural contexts. Any L&D design that requires extended time away from the site, assumes access to good internet connectivity, or relies on written materials as the primary medium will fail in practice. Learning design must start with the context of the G1 trainee in Myanmar or the G3 driller in Laos — not with a standard corporate learning design template.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: Myanmar coaching programme is 6 weeks, modular, delivered at the site during shift changes. Not a 3-day residential workshop. Not an online course. Designed for the context.
P3
Capability is observable — not assumed from completion
A G2 driller who has completed an 18-month programme is not automatically a G3. A G4 who has attended a coaching workshop does not automatically coach well. Progression is earned by demonstrating observable capability against defined milestones — not by time served or course completion. The L&D system must define what "competent at G3" looks like in practice, and give SMs and G4s the tools to assess it consistently.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: G4 milestone includes a trainee conversion rate metric, not just a tenure requirement. The G4 who has been in role 2 years but whose trainees consistently leave at month 3 has not demonstrated G4 capability.
P4
Measure outcomes, not inputs
Training hours delivered is not an L&D metric. Number of people trained is not an L&D metric. These are activity counts — they tell the COO what Group HR has been doing, not whether it is working. The metrics that matter at BaKuu Holding are: trainee conversion rate, time-to-competence at each grade milestone, check-in completion rate, and month-3 attrition rate. These are the numbers that connect L&D investment to business outcomes.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: The monthly HR data submission includes conversion rate and check-in completion — not training hours. The COO sees the business impact of L&D, not the activity level.
P5
Build capability to develop others — not just individuals
At BaKuu Holding's scale, Group HR cannot be the primary learning delivery mechanism. The HRBP's highest-leverage L&D investment is in the people who develop others — G4 drillers who develop G1–G3 trainees, SMs who develop G4s, and the Ops Lead who will develop SMs. Investing in a G4's coaching capability multiplies its impact across every trainee that G4 will ever work with. This is the principle that justifies the Myanmar coaching programme as the top L&D priority.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: 3 Myanmar G4 supervisors trained → expected to develop 8 trainees per cohort × 3 cohorts per year = 72 trainee development experiences improved per year. ROI per coaching programme hour is very high.
P6
Consistent standards, contextual delivery
The G3 capability milestone must be the same in Myanmar as it is in Indonesia — otherwise grade integrity breaks down and the pipeline is not comparable across countries. But the way that milestone is delivered and assessed can and should be contextualised — language, cultural communication norms, local operational context. This principle protects both consistency (the standard) and relevance (the delivery). SMs who request country-specific exceptions to milestone criteria are requesting a standards compromise, not a contextual adaptation.
Applied at BaKuu Holding: Group HR sets the milestone criteria (consistent). SMs deliver the development experience in their local context (contextual). The assessment is conducted by Group HR against the group standard (consistent).
