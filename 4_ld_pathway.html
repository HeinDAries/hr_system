L&D Tool 4 / 7 — G1→G4 Development Pathway

The Driller
Pathway

The G1→G4 pathway is BaKuu Holding's most critical business process. Every unit of exploration revenue depends on having enough G4 lead drillers to run operations across four countries. This tool defines exactly what a driller must demonstrate at each grade — and how Group HR and site managers ensure the pipeline stays full.

BaKuu Holding Pipeline Reality — March 2026
48–60 months from G1 entry to G4 certification. Every cohort matters.
The full G1→G4 journey takes 4–5 years under normal conditions. At BaKuu Holding's current scale, each G1 cohort of 8 trainees produces 1–2 G4-ready drillers over that timeline. Three Laos G4s are retiring within 3 years. Indonesia has 3 G3 vacancies open now. Myanmar is converting at 58% — 12pp below target. The pathway tool makes the pipeline visible, milestone-driven, and managed — not left to chance and individual G4 quality.

How the Pathway Works
The pathway is milestone-based, not time-based. A driller advances when they have demonstrated the required capabilities at their current grade — not simply because they have been in role for the expected number of months. Time estimates are indicative; milestone completion is mandatory.
Milestones, not months. Each grade has 3–5 defined milestones. Advancement requires all milestones at the current grade to be completed and signed off. A G2 who has been in role 18 months but has not completed milestone G2-M3 does not advance.
G4 sign-off, SM approval. Each milestone is signed off by the G4 (direct observation) and approved by the SM (quality check). Group HR reviews all G3→G4 transitions before Ba countersigns. This creates a three-level quality gate.
Quarterly pipeline review. Ba reviews all active candidates in the quarterly talent review — milestone completion, time-in-grade, G4 coaching quality, at-risk flags. Any candidate stalled for 3+ months on the same milestone triggers an intervention review.

Section 02 — Grade Profile

G1 — Trainee
Driller

The G1 Trainee Driller is the entry point to the BaKuu Holding pipeline. The G1 stage is the highest-attrition period — month 3 is the primary exit window. The primary development relationship is with the G4, and the primary retention mechanism is the quality of that relationship and the clarity of the development promise.

Role at G1
The G1 is an observer and learner. They do not make independent operational decisions. Their job is to absorb and demonstrate — watch, assist, ask, and practice. Every task is a learning event. The G4 is accountable for creating those learning events deliberately, not leaving development to chance exposure.
G1 Milestones — Required for G2 Advancement
All five milestones must be completed and signed off before the G1 can advance to G2. G4 signs each milestone based on direct observation. SM reviews and approves. Ba countersigns for the G1-M5 people milestone only.
M1
Site Safety and HSE Competency
Foundation certification — Month 1–2
Foundation+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Correct use of all required PPE without prompting. Knowledge of site emergency procedures — can locate and describe each response. Understanding of BaKuu Holding HSE incident reporting process. Zero unreported near-misses or safety violations in prior 4 weeks.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 direct observation over minimum 4-week period. G4 asks verbal questions about emergency procedures — trainee demonstrates without script. One formal site walkthrough with G4 to verify PPE and procedure knowledge. G4 signs milestone card.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
PPE compliance drops when G4 is not present (not yet internalised). Emergency procedure knowledge is scripted but not understood (passes verbal but fails unexpected scenario). Near-misses not reported due to fear of consequences — build psychological safety explicitly.
SIGN-OFF REQUIREMENT
G4 signature on milestone card. SM review. No countersignature from Group HR required — this is a site-level safety milestone.
M2
Equipment Familiarisation and Basic Operation
Technical foundation — Month 2–5
Technical+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Can identify all site equipment by name and function. Can perform daily equipment checks independently and document correctly. Can operate primary drilling equipment under G4 supervision without intervention for routine tasks. Understands equipment maintenance schedule and can explain rationale.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 uses "watch and narrate → assisted execution → supervised independent" progression (Tool 3 design framework). Milestone is signed when the G4 can step back to supervised independent on routine tasks without operational risk. Minimum 3 independent equipment checks completed and verified.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
G4 advances trainee to independent too early — operational risk. Trainee knows procedure but cannot explain rationale — surface learning only, will fail in non-routine situations. Equipment check documentation is completed but not reviewed by G4 — quality drops when not observed.
SIGN-OFF REQUIREMENT
G4 signature. SM verification that G4 observation was genuine (spot-check of G4 documentation). Not a written exam — demonstrated competency only.
M3
Drilling Fundamentals — Routine Operations
Core technical competency — Month 4–10
Technical+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Executes standard drilling programme sequences independently with G4 available. Maintains correct parameters (speed, pressure, angle) for geological conditions encountered. Completes shift handover correctly — written record, verbal debrief to G4 or G2. Identifies and reports deviations from expected geological conditions.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 reviews 10 consecutive shift records for parameter compliance and documentation quality. G4 conducts debrief on 3 shifts chosen without advance notice. Trainee must be able to explain decisions made during each shift, not just record that they happened. SM spot-checks one shift per month.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
Compliant in routine conditions but uncertain in variation — passes M3 too early, fails in G2 independent conditions. Shift records are filled in retrospectively rather than in real time. Trainee cannot explain why they made a parameter decision — execution without understanding.
SIGN-OFF REQUIREMENT
G4 signature based on shift record review and debrief quality. SM approval. This milestone typically takes longest — 4–6 months from start. Do not rush.
M4
Non-Routine Problem Response
Situational competency — Month 9–15
Advanced+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Responds correctly to at least 2 documented non-routine situations (equipment fault, unexpected geological condition, weather event). Knows escalation thresholds — when to handle independently vs when to call the G4. Does not panic or freeze. Documents the event and their decision process.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
Based on documented real events — not simulated. G4 reviews the trainee's incident/event documentation and debriefs each one using the four-question framework. The quality of the trainee's reflection (Q2) and principle formation (Q3) is the primary assessment signal. G4 must have 2 documented events to sign off.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
Trainee always escalates — even events they could handle. This is a confidence and decision-rights gap, not a capability gap. G4 must explicitly grant "attempt first" authority for a defined range of situations. Waiting for naturally occurring non-routine events can delay M4 — G4 should deliberately introduce low-risk variation.
SIGN-OFF REQUIREMENT
G4 signature with 2 documented events attached. SM review of event documentation. The quality of documentation is itself a milestone signal — poor documentation at M4 predicts difficulty at G2 independent level.
M5
Peer Support — Teaching Readiness
People capability signal — Month 12–18
People+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has successfully briefed a newer G1 on at least one task they have mastered — demonstrating, explaining, and checking for understanding. Can answer a newer G1's technical question accurately. G4 has observed this interaction directly at least once. This is the first signal of G4 potential — the trainee who teaches well is the future G4 candidate.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 observes at least one peer teaching interaction and debriefs both the teaching G1 and the learning G1 afterwards. Did the learning G1 understand? Did the teaching G1 check for understanding rather than just demonstrating? This is the first use of Kolb's cycle assessment at G1 level.
STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE
M5 is the earliest G4 pipeline signal. A G1 who completes M5 with strong quality is flagged in the quarterly talent review as a G4 candidate — this starts the long-term tracking 3+ years before the G3→G4 transition. Do not miss this signal.
SIGN-OFF REQUIREMENT
G4 signature with observation note. SM approval. Ba countersignature — Group HR records this milestone as a pipeline quality signal and flags strong performers in the quarterly talent review.

A G1 who enters with clear expectations, an assigned G4, and a visible development plan is significantly more likely to reach the month-3 check-in as an engaged trainee. A G1 who is dropped into site operations without structure is already at risk.

Section 03 — Grade Profile

G2 — Driller

The G2 Driller has demonstrated foundational competency and is now developing operational independence. The G2 stage is characterised by progressive autonomy — the G4 steps back, the G2 steps up. The key development signal at G2 is how the driller behaves when the G4 is not watching.

Grade Specification
GRADE
G2
TYPICAL DURATION
12–18 months
PRIMARY DEVELOPMENT RELATIONSHIP
G4 (oversight) · G3 where available (peer learning)
KEY DEVELOPMENT SIGNAL
Behaviour when G4 is not present
PROGRESSION TARGET
≥75% progress to G3
INDONESIA CONTEXT
3 G3 vacancies create pressure on G2 pipeline — accelerated pathway for top performers
Role at G2
The G2 executes standard operations independently. They are no longer observing and assisting — they are doing. The G4's role shifts from active coach to available supervisor. The G2 makes operational decisions within defined parameters and escalates only when those parameters are exceeded. They begin to see themselves as drillers, not trainees.
G2 Milestones — Required for G3 Advancement
Four milestones required for G2→G3 advancement. The G2 stage develops two parallel tracks: advanced technical competency and early people leadership. Both must be demonstrated. Technical excellence alone is not sufficient for G3 advancement.
M1
Independent Operations — Complex Conditions
Advanced technical — Months 1–8 at G2
Technical+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Manages a full shift independently — including at least one non-routine event — without G4 intervention. Adapts drilling parameters to different geological formations without direction. Completes programme sequences in variable conditions. G4's role is remote availability, not active supervision.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 reviews shift records for 8 consecutive independent shifts. Debriefs 3 shifts in depth. SM spot-checks one shift record per 6-week period. The G4 specifically looks for evidence of adaptive decision-making — changes made for good operational reasons, documented and explained.
INDONESIA ACCELERATED NOTE
For the Indonesia G3 gap: identify G2 drillers who are approaching M1 completion. These are the accelerated pipeline candidates. Do not advance them prematurely — but do prioritise their G4 coaching time to accelerate M1 to M4 completion within 10–12 months rather than 12–18.
SIGN-OFF
G4 signature. SM approval. Shift records retained for Group HR quarterly review.
M2
Equipment Maintenance and Fault Diagnosis
Technical mastery — Months 4–12 at G2
Technical+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Can perform scheduled maintenance on primary drilling equipment independently. Can diagnose common faults — describe the fault, its likely cause, and the correct response — without reference to the manual. Has managed at least one equipment fault situation: identified, documented, escalated or resolved, and debriefed.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 observes one scheduled maintenance session without assistance. G4 conducts a verbal fault diagnosis assessment — presents 3 fault scenarios, G2 diagnoses each. One documented real fault event required. G4 rates: "Would I be comfortable with this G2 handling this without me available?" If no — M2 is not complete.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
G2 can perform maintenance correctly but cannot explain why each step matters. Surface competency is not M2. The maintenance knowledge must be transferable — a G2 who cannot explain maintenance to a G1 will not complete G3-M3 (training design capability).
SIGN-OFF
G4 signature with fault documentation attached. SM review.
M3
G1 Trainee Day-to-Day Supervision
People leadership — Months 8–15 at G2
People+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has supervised a G1 trainee through a full work shift — assigning tasks, monitoring execution, providing real-time correction, and briefing the G4 at end of shift. The G4 is available but not present on the floor. The G2's debrief to the G4 covers: what the G1 did well, what needed correction, and what the G2 will do differently next time they supervise.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 observes the G2-G1 interaction from distance on at least 2 occasions. G4 debriefs the G2 using the four-question framework after each supervision session. G4 also separately debriefs the G1: "How was working with [G2]? Did you understand what you were asked to do?" The G1's experience of the G2's supervision is part of the milestone assessment.
STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE
M3 quality predicts G4 capability more than any other G2 milestone. A G2 who cannot give clear direction to a G1, or who cannot debrief their own supervision experience with self-awareness, will struggle at G3→G4. Flag strong M3 performers as G4 pathway candidates in the quarterly talent review.
SIGN-OFF
G4 signature with 2 observation notes attached. SM review. Ba notified of strong M3 performers at next quarterly talent review.
M4
Operational Planning and Shift Leadership
Leadership readiness — Months 12–18 at G2
Leadership+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Plans and executes a shift with a team of G1 trainees — assigns work, manages time and sequence, responds to unexpected events, and completes the shift to programme targets. Presents the shift plan to the G4 before execution and the shift summary after. Has identified and communicated one efficiency or quality improvement observed during a shift.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 reviews the pre-shift plan and post-shift summary. G4 interviews the G1 team members: did they understand the plan? Did the G2 adjust when things changed? G4 assesses: is this G2 ready to run a shift without me available? This is the G3 readiness gate — G4 should only sign M4 when the honest answer is yes.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
G2 plans well but loses the plan under pressure — reverts to task-by-task reaction. The shift plan is a thinking tool; if the G2 abandons it when conditions change, they are not ready for G3 independence. The G4 should introduce a planned disruption during the observed shift to test adaptive planning.
SIGN-OFF
G4 signature. SM approval with specific note: "This G2 is ready for G3 independent operation." Group HR receives G2→G3 promotion recommendation with all four milestone cards attached.
Section 04 — Grade Profile

G3 — Senior
Driller

The G3 Senior Driller is the most operationally capable driller grade. G3s run complex operations, manage G1 trainees, and are the primary operational resource at each site. The G3→G4 transition is the hardest in the pipeline — it requires not just technical mastery but a demonstrated shift from doing to developing others.

The G3→G4 development cycle at BaKuu Holding takes 18–24 months under normal conditions. Without June 2026 activation, the net Laos G4 deficit by 2027 is unavoidable.

The G3→G4 Shift
The G3→G4 transition is the hardest promotion at BaKuu Holding because it requires a fundamental shift in identity — from "I am the best driller" to "I make other drillers better." Many technically excellent G3s fail this transition not because they lack capability, but because the G4 role has never been clearly defined as a development role with explicit accountability for trainee conversion rate.
G3 Milestones — Required for G4 Advancement
Five milestones required. The final two are people-focused and are the primary G4 readiness signals. A G3 who completes M1–M3 but not M4–M5 is not G4-ready — regardless of technical excellence.
M1
Technical Mastery — Non-Standard Operations
Advanced technical — Months 1–10 at G3
Technical+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has successfully led complex or non-standard drilling operations — unusual geological conditions, programme deviation, multi-phase or multi-shift operation. Has made technical decisions under pressure that deviated from standard procedure for good operational reasons, and can fully justify those decisions.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
SM review of 3 documented non-standard operations — written record plus verbal debrief with G4 or SM. The debrief specifically tests: did the G3 know when to deviate and when to escalate? The boundary between autonomous G3 decision and SM escalation is the core competency at this milestone.
CROSS-SITE EXPOSURE VALUE
A 4–6 week cross-site exposure to Thailand or Indonesia accelerates M1 by exposing the G3 to geological and operational conditions different from their home site. Laos G3 candidates should have cross-site exposure built into their development plan — this both accelerates M1 and satisfies the G4 readiness "independent in new context" requirement.
SIGN-OFF
SM signature (not G4 — SM is the appropriate assessor at this level of technical complexity). Group HR review at next quarterly talent review.
M2
Programme Management and Reporting
Operational leadership — Months 6–14 at G3
Operational+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Manages a multi-week drilling programme segment — plans the sequence, tracks daily progress against programme targets, identifies deviations, adjusts the plan, and reports to the SM. Produces a written programme summary that the SM can use without needing to ask follow-up questions.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
SM reviews one complete programme segment managed by the G3 — plan, daily records, summary report. SM rates the report: "Can I use this to brief the client or the COO without asking clarifying questions?" If yes — M2 is complete. This milestone directly prepares the G3 for the G4 programme reporting accountability.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
G3 tracks what happened but not why, and what the response was. Reports that describe events without analysis or decision rationale do not meet M2 standard. The G4 coaching programme's debrief discipline (Q2 and Q3) is the precursor capability that M2 builds on.
SIGN-OFF
SM signature with programme summary attached. Group HR receives copy for pipeline file.
M3
Technical Training Design and Delivery
Teaching capability — Months 8–16 at G3
Teaching+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has designed and delivered a structured learning session for G1 trainees — not just assigned tasks, but planned what the trainee would learn, how they would learn it, and how the G3 would know they had learned it. Has demonstrated at least one teaching session using the watch-and-narrate → assisted execution → debrief progression from Tool 3.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
G4 or SM observes one G3-led teaching session. Assesses: did the G3 use the four learning modes? Did the session end with a specific commitment from the G1? Could the G3 articulate what the G1 had learned at the end of the session? The G4 coaching programme content (Tool 3) is the reference framework — G3 should have been exposed to it before M3 assessment.
LINK TO G4 COACHING PROGRAMME
G3 candidates approaching M3 should be invited to observe or co-facilitate one session of the G4 coaching programme. This exposes them to the framework before they need to apply it at G4 level — accelerating their own M3 progress and seeding their future coaching approach.
SIGN-OFF
G4 or SM signature with observation note. Ba reviews at quarterly talent review — strong M3 performance triggers formal G4 candidacy flag.
M4
G1 Trainee Development Accountability
Primary G4 readiness signal — Months 12–22 at G3
Development+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has taken primary development responsibility for a G1 trainee through their first 6 months — including running the month-3 check-in. The G1 trainee must have progressed past month 3 without attrition. G3 has produced written development updates to the SM at month 1, month 3, and month 6. G3 has raised at least one at-risk concern proactively — before the G1's behaviour became a departure risk.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
SM reviews all three development updates. Ba assesses the check-in quality using the month-3 check-in documentation framework (Tool 3). The G1's outcome — progressed past month 3 — is the primary success indicator. A G3 whose G1 trainee leaves at month 3 without the G3 having raised any at-risk signals has not completed M4, regardless of other factors.
LAOS URGENCY NOTE
For Laos G3 candidates: M4 must be in progress by June 2026 — meaning the G3 must be actively developing a G1 trainee with Group HR oversight by that date. If no G1 is currently assigned, Ba must arrange the assignment in coordination with the Laos SM immediately.
SIGN-OFF
SM signature. Ba countersignature — Group HR must be involved in G4 readiness assessments. Three development updates attached. Check-in documentation attached.
M5
Performance and Conduct Management — G1 Level
People management proof — Months 16–24 at G3
People Mgmt+
WHAT MUST BE DEMONSTRATED
Has managed at least one performance or conduct situation with a G1 trainee — lateness, quality issue, attitude concern — using the developmental feedback approach (Tool 3). Has documented the situation, the conversation, and the outcome. Has escalated to the SM when the situation exceeded G3 authority. Has not ignored a performance issue because it felt uncomfortable.
ASSESSMENT METHOD
SM debriefs the G3 on the documented performance situation: "What did you do? Why? What happened? What would you do differently?" The quality of the G3's reflection — not just the outcome — is the assessment criterion. A G3 who handled a situation perfectly but cannot articulate why is less ready for G4 than a G3 who made a mistake and can fully debrief it.
COMMON FAILURE MODES
G3 avoids the performance conversation because it is uncomfortable. This is a very common G3 failure mode — technically strong, people-avoidant. A G3 who does not complete M5 is not G4-ready, because the G4 role includes managing G1 attendance, performance, and exit conversations. The G4 who avoids these conversations is the G4 whose G1 attrition rate stays at 58%.
SIGN-OFF
SM signature with documented performance situation attached. Ba countersignature. All five G3 milestones complete → G4 promotion recommendation to Group HR and COO.
Section 05 — Grade Profile

G4 — Lead
Driller

The G4 Lead Driller is the most strategically important role at BaKuu Holding below Site Manager. The G4's technical capability keeps programmes running. The G4's coaching quality determines whether the next generation of drillers exists. The G4 is accountable for both.

The G4 Accountability Shift
The G4 is not the best driller. The G4 is the person who creates the next best drillers.
This distinction is the single most important framing in the G4 role profile. G4s hired or promoted because of technical excellence frequently default to the mindset that their job is to drill — and that trainee development is a secondary administrative burden. The G4 coaching programme (Tool 3), the G4 performance metrics below, and this pathway tool all exist to make the people development accountability primary and explicit, not secondary and aspirational.

Section 06 — Transition Decision Framework

Transition Decision
Framework

Grade transitions at BaKuu Holding are decisions, not events. They require deliberate assessment, multi-level sign-off, and a clear rationale. This section gives Ba and SMs the framework for making consistent, defensible promotion decisions — and for managing situations where a candidate is stalled or at risk of regression.

The Promotion Decision Criteria
Every promotion decision at BaKuu Holding is evaluated against four criteria. All four must be satisfied before a promotion recommendation is made. A candidate who meets three of four is not ready — they have a defined gap that needs a defined intervention.
CRITERION 1 — MILESTONES
All grade milestones completed and signed off
Every milestone for the current grade must have a G4 or SM signature and a completion date. Partial completion of a milestone does not count. No exceptions — if a milestone is incomplete, the promotion is deferred until it is complete.
CRITERION 2 — OBSERVATION
SM has directly observed next-grade behaviour
The SM must be able to state: "I have personally observed this candidate behaving at the next grade level — not just reaching the current grade standard." For G3→G4 this means the SM has seen the candidate run a development conversation. For G1→G2 this means independent operation without G4 prompting.
CRITERION 3 — TIME IN GRADE
Minimum time-in-grade satisfied
G1: minimum 12 months. G2: minimum 12 months. G3: minimum 18 months. These minimums exist because milestone completion before the minimum time often reflects G4 sign-off quality rather than candidate readiness. A candidate completing all G1 milestones in 8 months should be reviewed carefully — are the milestones genuinely complete, or was the G4 advancing them prematurely?
CRITERION 4 — GROUP HR REVIEW
Group HR pipeline review completed
Ba reviews all promotion recommendations at the quarterly talent review. For G3→G4, Ba countersigns the promotion recommendation before it goes to COO. This is not a veto — it is a quality check. Ba reviews milestone documentation, checks for consistency across countries, and confirms the promotion against the pipeline plan. Any concern raised by Ba must be resolved before the promotion proceeds.
The accelerated pathway exception — Indonesia G3 contextIn extraordinary circumstances (Indonesia G3 vacancy crisis), a G2 candidate may be advanced before full time-in-grade if milestones 1–4 are complete, SM has observed next-grade behaviour, and Ba has approved in writing. This exception must be documented and the candidate's development plan must include completion of any outstanding elements within 6 months of promotion. It is an exception — not a standard practice.
Stalled Candidate Protocol
A candidate who has not progressed on any milestone in 3+ consecutive months is considered stalled. Stalled candidates require a diagnostic conversation before any further development investment is made.

The Promotion Conversation — What to Say
The promotion conversation is a development moment, not an HR administrative event. The SM and G4 together have the conversation with the candidate — framing what the promotion means, what the new expectations are, and what the candidate must do to succeed at the next grade.
WHAT THE CONVERSATION INCLUDES
What the promotion recognises. What is different at the new grade — accountabilities, expectations, and freedoms. What the new pay step is (confirm with Ba before the conversation — no surprises). What the first 90-day development focus will be at the new grade. Who their development relationship is with at the new grade. What the next milestone target is.
WHAT THE CONVERSATION DOES NOT INCLUDE
A general welcome and congratulations with no development content. An announcement without a conversation. A pay change communication without role expectation clarity. Any suggestion that the promoted person has "arrived" — every grade is a foundation for the next, not a destination.

Myanmar — Pathway Profile
Priority: fix G1→G2 conversion. Address month-3 attrition before anything else.
Myanmar is BaKuu Holding's highest-volume trainee country and its highest-attrition context. The 58% conversion rate is 12pp below target and costs $18K–$36K per year in avoidable replacement cost. The pathway adaptation for Myanmar focuses on the G1 stage — tightening the first 90 days, activating the month-3 check-in, and addressing the G4 coaching quality gap through the coaching programme.
Myanmar Adaptations — G1 Stage
Month-3 check-in is mandatory and monitored. Ba tracks Myanmar check-in completion in the monthly HR report. Any check-in missed triggers a direct follow-up with the Myanmar SM within 48 hours.
Language adaptation. All G1 milestone cards and check-in documentation are in Myanmar language. G4 coaching frameworks are delivered in Myanmar language via the coaching programme.
Family pressure acknowledgement. Myanmar induction explicitly acknowledges family expectations and the long-term nature of the G1→G4 pathway. Trainees are asked at induction: "What does your family expect from this job?" — and this is incorporated into their development plan framing.
G4 coaching programme priority. All 3 Myanmar G4 supervisors complete the G4 coaching programme by June 2026. Conversion rate is reviewed at Q3 2026 as the primary measure of programme impact.

Indonesia — Pathway Profile
Priority: fill G3 vacancies, then build the G2→G3 pipeline to prevent recurrence.
Indonesia has 3 G3 vacancies open 40+ days. Programme delivery has dropped from 93% to 84%. External sourcing is the immediate response, but the structural fix is building a G2→G3 pipeline that produces G3s internally within 12–15 months. The Indonesia SM's resistance to Group HR involvement is a governance risk that Ba must manage carefully — the pipeline work must proceed regardless.
Indonesia Adaptations — G2→G3 Accelerated Pipeline
Emergency sourcing first. 3 G3 external hires needed by May 2026. Group HR leads the process. Indonesia SM is hiring manager. Delivery recovery cannot wait for the pipeline fix — both must run in parallel.
G2 accelerated pathway identification. Ba reviews all Indonesia G2 drillers for M1–M3 completion status. Any G2 who is ≥60% through their milestones is identified as an accelerated candidate. G4 coaching time prioritised for these candidates.
Labour law compliance. Indonesia has stricter employment regulations. All promotion decisions — and any performance management that might lead to exit — must involve Group HR review before any action. The Indonesia SM must not proceed unilaterally.
SM engagement strategy. Present Thailand data at Q2 peer forum: Thailand's pipeline practices → higher conversion rate → lower vacancy risk. Data, not compliance pressure, is the Indonesia SM influence mechanism. COO endorsement of Group HR pipeline authority is the backstop.

Thailand — Pathway Profile
Priority: document what is working and transfer it. Thailand is the pipeline benchmark.
Thailand operates at 105% country factor, the highest conversion rates in the group, and the strongest G4 coaching quality. The pathway priority for Thailand is not fixing gaps — it is documenting and transferring what works before it becomes a single-point-of-failure knowledge risk. If the Thailand SM moves to Country 5, the practices leave with them unless they are captured first.
Thailand Adaptations — Knowledge Capture Priority
Practice capture Q2 2026. Ba conducts the Thailand SM structured interview (5 questions, Tool 3 Section 04). Output: 3–5 documented practices for peer forum transfer. Thailand SM presents first at every peer forum — their practices are the reference for the group.
Cross-site host role formalised. Thailand is the preferred host for cross-site exposure placements from Laos, Myanmar, and Indonesia. Thailand SM explicitly included in cross-site candidate briefings — "you are a development resource for the group."
Country 5 succession planning. If Thailand SM is the Country 5 candidate (Q3 2026 assessment), Thailand G4 succession must be activated simultaneously. Thailand pipeline must not be sacrificed for Country 5 gain.
G4 coaching programme co-facilitation. Thailand SM co-facilitates modules 2, 4, and 6 of the Myanmar G4 coaching programme. This is both a transfer mechanism and a recognition of Thailand's benchmark status.
