Compliance & Legal — Contract Clauses
EMPLOYMENT CONTRACT FRAMEWORK · ALL COUNTRIES
Confidential · HR Only
Universal contract clauses · customise per country below
These are framework clauses — not final legal text. All employment contracts must be reviewed by a qualified labour lawyer in each operating country before signing. BaKuu Holding currently operates under 4 different national labour law regimes.
§
Core Clauses — All Contracts
Required in every employment contract regardless of country
Parties & Commencement
Full legal name of employing entity, employee full name and national ID/passport number, and confirmed start date. Critical: the employing entity must be the legal entity registered in the country of work — not the Laos parent company.
Role & Reporting Line
Job title, primary duties summary, reporting line, and work location (including "or such other location as the Company may reasonably require"). Do not over-specify duties — leaves room for operational flexibility.
Compensation
Base salary (state currency), payment date, method (bank transfer), and breakdown of any allowances as separate line items. Do not bundle allowances into base — this affects statutory calculations.
Working Hours & Rotation
For site-based roles: state the rotation schedule explicitly (e.g. "8 weeks on site / 3 weeks off"). Failure to document rotation has created successful overtime claims in SEA jurisdictions.
Probation Period
Length of probation per country statute. Company's right to terminate during probation with shortened notice. Confirmation of employment process at end of probation.
Confidentiality
Covers: operational data, client/government relationships, compensation information, and all proprietary methods. Survives termination. Critical for a small-team SEA exploration company where staff have access to site coordinates and mineralisation data.
Termination & Notice
Notice periods per country statute (see Country Law Guide). Grounds for summary dismissal: gross misconduct, safety violation, dishonesty, breach of confidentiality. Garden leave rights for senior roles.
+
Expat-Only Additional Clauses
Required for non-local national employees
Work Permit Condition
Employment is conditional upon the Company obtaining and maintaining a valid work permit. If permit is refused or revoked through no fault of the Company, the contract terminates without liability.
Expat Package Details
Housing allowance (monthly amount or Company-provided), R&R flights (number, class, routing entitlement per rotation), visa fees (fully Company-borne), medical insurance (coverage scope).
Tax Equalisation
Specify whether package is gross or net of local tax. For Myanmar and Laos where expat tax treatment varies — get written tax advice before issuing contract. Many companies state "employer assumes withholding tax obligations."
Repatriation
On contract end or termination (except gross misconduct), Company covers one-way economy repatriation flight to country of origin or last place of hire.
!
High-Risk Clauses to Watch
Common mistakes that create legal exposure
Non-Compete Clauses
Not enforceable in Thailand or Indonesia for most roles. Use carefully — a broad non-compete may be unenforceable and signals poor drafting to candidates.
Fixed-Term Contracts
Repeated renewal of fixed-term contracts can trigger permanent employment status under Thai and Indonesian law. Track renewal count — third renewal typically creates permanent status.
Salary in USD (Myanmar)
Myanmar law requires salary stated in MMK. USD can be referenced as a benchmark but official contract must show MMK equivalent. USD-only contracts are non-compliant.

Reference check guide · structured questions · documentation
◎
Process — Before You Call
Candidate Permission
Always get written consent before contacting references. Include in offer letter: "This offer is conditional upon satisfactory reference checks. By accepting, you consent to us contacting your nominated referees."
Who to Call
Minimum 2 references. At least one must be a direct line manager (not peer or subordinate). For Site Manager and Drilling Supervisor — require a reference from their most recent site operations role specifically.
When to Call
After Round 2 interview, before verbal offer. Never after written offer — you have no leverage if the reference is negative.
Document Everything
Notes from every reference call. Date, time, referee name and title, verbatim responses to key questions. Store in candidate file. This is legal protection if a hire fails probation.
⚑
Red Flags in Reference Calls
Referee is very brief, non-specific, or hedges constantly — "He was… fine." This is a managed response. Push for specifics.
Referee will not confirm dates or title accurately — discrepancy with what candidate told you. Verify before proceeding.
Long pause before answering "Would you rehire?" — often signals a managed exit or performance issue. Probe: "What would need to be different for you to rehire them?"
Referee is the candidate's peer or friend, not a manager. Insist on a direct line manager reference. Peer references are character references, not performance references.
Referee cannot speak to the candidate's specific role on site — suggests the candidate overstated their scope of responsibility.

Reference Check Question Bank
Use for all roles — adapt depth for seniority
Opening — Establish Context
01
"Can you confirm [Candidate's] exact title, reporting line, and employment dates?"
Cross-reference against CV. Discrepancies here are disqualifying.
02
"What was the nature of your working relationship — were you their direct manager?"
Ensure this is actually a line manager, not a friendly peer.
03
"In the role they held, what were their primary responsibilities?"
Listen for any narrowing of scope vs what the candidate claimed.
Performance
04
"How would you describe their performance overall — what stood out positively?"
05
"Were there areas where they struggled or received developmental feedback?"
Silence here is a signal. Press gently: "Everyone has areas to grow — what was theirs?"
06
"Can you give me an example of a challenging situation they handled well?"
Role-Specific (Ops)
07
"How did they manage their team on site — what was their leadership style?"
For Site Manager and Supervisor only. Listen for crew relationship quality.
08
"Were there any HSE incidents or near-misses during their tenure? How did they handle them?"
Non-negotiable question for any ops role. Any evasion here is a disqualifier.
Close
09
"Why did they leave — and what were the circumstances?"
Confirm the story matches what the candidate told you. Minor variances are normal; major ones are not.
10
"Would you rehire them if you had the opportunity — and in what type of role?"
The most predictive question in any reference call. A hesitation or qualified answer is as informative as a direct no.
11
"Is there anything else you'd want me to know as we consider them for a senior site role?"
Open-ended close. Occasionally surfaces critical information not covered by specific questions.

Probation framework · check-ins · confirmation · underperformance
◷
Probation Check-In Schedule
HR-led · documented · mandatory
Day 30 — Settling-In Check
Led by: HR Manager. Format: 30-min informal call or in-person. Focus: Is the employee settling in? Any blockers — access, equipment, team integration? Log any concerns now. This is the earliest useful data point.
Document: Brief summary email to file. Not a formal review.
Day 60 — Mid-Probation Review
Led by: HR + Hiring Manager together. Format: structured conversation against role expectations. If performance concerns exist — raise them NOW in writing. This is the decision point. Continuing to probation end without raising issues means you cannot terminate for those issues.
Document: Signed mid-probation review form. Copy to employee. Copy to file.
Day 85–90 — Confirmation Decision
HR drafts confirmation letter or extension/termination letter. Decision must be communicated BEFORE probation end — not on the last day. Decision options: (1) Confirm employment, (2) Extend probation up to statutory maximum, (3) Terminate with probation notice.
Document: Signed confirmation or termination letter. Labour authority notification if required by country.
⚑
Underperformance During Probation
Step 1 — Verbal Conversation
Specific, documented conversation about the performance gap. "Here is what we expected. Here is what we're seeing. Here is what needs to change and by when."
Step 2 — Written Warning
If no improvement after verbal: written warning with specific improvement targets and a defined improvement window (usually 2–4 weeks within probation).
Step 3 — Termination (if warranted)
With documented evidence trail from Steps 1–2, terminate with probation notice period. This is legally defensible. Terminating at Day 89 with zero documentation is not.

What NOT to Over-Promise
Employer brand requires honesty — over-promise now = early attrition later
Don't over-romanticise remote site life. Candidates who didn't understand what remote SEA operations actually meant are the ones who leave in the first 90 days. Be clear: limited connectivity, shared facilities, mostly male workforce.
Don't promise rapid promotion without clarity. "Growth opportunities" without a specific path is a cliché. Either define what advancement looks like at BaKuu Holding or don't mention it.
Don't misrepresent the HR function maturity. For HRBP/HR Manager candidates — be honest that the function is in build mode. The right person will be attracted by that. The wrong person will leave disappointed.
LinkedIn presence playbook · 6-step build for BaKuu Holding
1
Optimise the Company Page
BaKuu Holding needs a complete, professional LinkedIn company page before any outreach. Incomplete pages undermine credibility — candidates check before responding. Required: high-res logo, banner image (site operations photo works well), tagline, about section, and website link.
About section formula: "BaKuu Holding is an active mineral exploration company operating across Southeast Asia — [countries]. We run [X] drill sites across [Y] countries with a multinational team of [Z] professionals. We're building the infrastructure and talent base for the next phase of regional exploration."
2
Establish Your Personal HR Brand Profile
As Group HR Manager, your personal LinkedIn profile is the face of BaKuu Holding talent acquisition. Candidates research who reached out to them. A sparse or generic profile reduces response rates significantly.
Headline: "Group HR Manager · BaKuu Holding · Mineral Exploration · SEA Operations · Building the team behind Southeast Asia's next generation of exploration sites."
3
Post Content 2–3× Per Month (Company Page)
Consistent content builds passive awareness in the drilling and mining community. Candidates who follow your content before you approach them respond at 2–3× higher rates. You don't need high production quality — authenticity works better in this sector.
Content types that work for mining/exploration: Site operations updates (with permission), team culture moments, industry insights on SEA exploration, career opportunity posts with real context about the role and lifestyle.
4
Activate the Internal Network First
Before external outreach: ask current staff to follow the company page, like posts, and share when relevant. A company page with 3 followers looks inactive. 50 followers from the internal team gives immediate social proof to candidates who check.
5
Personalised Outreach — Non-Negotiable
Template outreach with zero personalisation gets ~10% response rates. Personalised outreach that references something specific from the candidate's profile gets 30–40%. In the tight-knit drilling community, generic messages also damage reputation.
Personalisation minimum: Reference one specific thing — a project they worked on, a certification they hold, a company they've worked for in a relevant region. "I came across your profile and noticed your work on rotary drilling programs in Cambodia — that's exactly the operational context we're building for in Laos."
6
Track Engagement — What's Working
Monthly: review which posts drove the most profile views and follower growth. Quarterly: count inbound connection requests from relevant profiles — this is passive brand-building working. Any inbound candidate who followed BaKuu Holding before applying should be fast-tracked through screening — they're already bought in.

Employer brand messaging · tone guide · ready-to-use copy
◎
Tone of Voice — BaKuu Holding Employer Brand
✓ Direct and specific
Say what the job actually is. Say where the site actually is. Say what the package actually includes. Vague employer branding wastes everyone's time.
✓ Honest about the challenge
Remote. Southeast Asia. Small team. High accountability. Say these things. The right candidates are attracted by challenge, not despite it.
✓ Human, not corporate
No "synergies", "dynamic environments", or "passionate teams". Real language. The drilling community has a low tolerance for corporate speak.
✗ Avoid: generic claims
"We value our people." Everyone says this. If you value your people, show it with specifics: how you structure rotation, how you handle welfare on remote sites, what development looks like.
✗ Avoid: overselling
Don't promise what BaKuu Holding can't yet deliver. Employer brand integrity is built over time — one overpromise that a candidate discovers after joining costs more than not attracting them.
✗ Avoid: passive job postings
"Send your CV to hr@valentis.com" with nothing else is not an employer brand. A job post is a pitch. The candidate is evaluating you as much as you are evaluating them.
