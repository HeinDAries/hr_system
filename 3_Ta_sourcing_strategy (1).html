The Core Sourcing Rule
Applied to every role, every time
Source before you need
For every critical role, maintain 2–3 warm candidates at all times — even when there's no vacancy. The Drilling Supervisor crisis exists because sourcing only started when the vacancy opened.
The 3-channel rule
For any active role, always run at least 3 sourcing channels simultaneously. Never rely on a single job post. LinkedIn + referral + specialist channel is the minimum.
Referral is the best channel
In SEA drilling, the best candidates don't apply to job ads. They are referred. Activate your network FIRST before posting anywhere — especially for Supervisor and Manager roles.

Operations roles · Site Manager · Drilling Supervisor · Senior Driller · Equipment Tech · Geologist
⚑
Why Ops Roles Are Hard to Source in SEA
Thin local pool. Myanmar and Laos have very limited qualified local drilling professionals at Supervisor level and above. Most qualified candidates are Central Asian (Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan), Australian, or African expatriates.
They don't job hunt. Experienced drilling supervisors are almost always employed. They don't scroll job boards. They move via referral, direct approach from previous colleagues, or headhunter contact.
Myanmar context premium. The political environment makes Myanmar less attractive. Expect to pay a 15–25% premium over comparable roles in more stable markets and to face higher rejection rates at offer stage.

Strategic Note — HR Roles
The HRBP Thailand role especially should be a collaborative hire — involve the Group HR Manager in all stages. This person will effectively be your deputy in Thailand. LinkedIn Boolean search strings · copy and paste into LinkedIn search
🔴 Site Manager — SEA Operations
("Site Manager" OR "Operations Manager" OR "Project Manager") AND ("drilling" OR "mineral exploration" OR "mining") AND ("Laos" OR "Myanmar" OR "Southeast Asia" OR "SEA") AND ("500" OR "headcount" OR "remote site" OR "field operations")
Tip: Filter by "Past 2 years" activity to exclude inactive profiles. Add Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, or Australia as location filters to find expat candidates with relevant backgrounds.
🔴 Drilling Supervisor — Myanmar (Urgent)
("Drilling Supervisor" OR "Drill Supervisor" OR "Senior Driller") AND ("mineral exploration" OR "diamond core" OR "rotary drilling" OR "RC drilling") AND ("Myanmar" OR "Southeast Asia" OR "remote site" OR "Mandalay") NOT "oil" NOT "petroleum"
Remove "NOT oil NOT petroleum" if expanding to oil & gas drillers who can transfer skills. Many rotary drilling supervisors from O&G can adapt to mineral exploration with brief upskilling.
🟠 Senior Driller — Indonesia/Kalimantan
("Senior Driller" OR "Diamond Core Driller" OR "Exploration Driller") AND ("Kalimantan" OR "Indonesia" OR "mineral exploration") AND ("gold" OR "nickel" OR "copper" OR "mineral") AND ("10 years" OR "experienced")
Filter by Indonesia location. Add Bahasa Indonesia language filter for local hires. For expat candidates, remove location filter and add "willing to relocate" as a keyword.
🟢 HSE Officer — SEA / Laos
("HSE Officer" OR "Safety Officer" OR "Health Safety Environment") AND ("NEBOSH" OR "IOSH" OR "safety certification") AND ("mining" OR "construction" OR "drilling" OR "oil gas") AND ("Thailand" OR "Laos" OR "Southeast Asia") AND ("site" OR "remote" OR "field")
Thai HSE professionals are particularly good targets — cultural proximity to Laos, often have cross-border experience, and there's a larger qualified pool than in Laos directly.
🟡 HRBP / HR Manager — Thailand
("HRBP" OR "HR Business Partner" OR "HR Manager") AND ("Thailand" OR "Bangkok") AND ("labor law" OR "Thai labor" OR "multi-country" OR "regional HR") AND ("mining" OR "construction" OR "manufacturing" OR "international company")
Filter by Bangkok location. Look for 5+ years experience in profile summary. Prioritize candidates who mention Thai Labor Protection Act or multi-country payroll — these are rare differentiators.
🔵 Payroll Officer — Multi-country SEA
("Payroll" OR "Payroll Officer" OR "Payroll Manager" OR "Compensation") AND ("multi-country" OR "regional payroll" OR "SEA payroll" OR "cross-border") AND ("Thailand" OR "Southeast Asia") AND ("SSO" OR "statutory" OR "labor compliance")
Multi-country payroll experience is rare. Cast a wider net — accept candidates with strong single-country payroll who show ability to learn. SSO and statutory compliance keywords help filter for Thai payroll practitioners.
🔵 Indonesia Compliance Officer
("HR Compliance" OR "Labor Compliance" OR "Legal HR" OR "Industrial Relations") AND ("Indonesia" OR "Kalimantan" OR "Jakarta") AND ("KITAS" OR "work permit" OR "IMTA" OR "Manpower Law" OR "UU Ketenagakerjaan") AND ("mining" OR "extractive" OR "plantation" OR "oil gas")
KITAS and IMTA keywords are highly specific — anyone with these in their profile has direct work permit experience. Filter by Indonesia location. Kalimantan-based candidates will have relevant site compliance experience.
Copy strings directly into LinkedIn search bar. Refine by: Connection degree (2nd and 3rd for broader reach), Location (per role), Industry (Mining & Metals, Oil & Gas for ops roles), and Activity (Past 30 days to avoid inactive profiles). Always personalize connection requests — generic requests get ~10% acceptance vs 35%+ for personalized notes.

When to Use an Agency
Agency fees are an investment, not a cost
Use immediately when:Role is overdue with no pipeline (Myanmar Supervisor). Critical operations role that cannot stay vacant. You have zero bandwidth for active sourcing.
Consider using when:Thin local talent pool (all ops roles in MM/LA). Confidential search. Role requires specific technical credentialing you can't verify independently.
Don't use agency for:Support/admin roles. Roles where you have warm bench candidates. Roles where the JD is unclear — fix the JD first or you'll waste the agency's time and yours.

Outreach message templates · LinkedIn + WhatsApp + email · copy and personalize
🔴 LinkedIn InMail — Drilling Supervisor (Urgent · Myanmar)
Subject: Drilling Supervisor — Myanmar Site · Competitive Package Hi [Name], Your background in [specific experience from profile] caught my attention. We're looking for a Drilling Supervisor for an active mineral exploration site in Mandalay Region, Myanmar. The role: Leading a rig crew of 15–30 in a rotary/diamond core drilling program. Reporting to Site Manager. Competitive base of $1,800–$3,000/mo + hardship premium + rotation schedule. You'd be working with a team that operates across SEA — Laos, Myanmar, Indonesia. The environment is remote and operationally demanding, which I suspect suits your background. Would a 15-minute call this week make sense? Happy to share full details. [Your name] Group HR Manager, Valentis Laos
Keep it short. Lead with their specific background, not a generic pitch. Mention the hardship premium upfront for Myanmar roles — it signals you understand the market. Never attach the JD in a first message — it kills response rate.
🔵 LinkedIn Connection Request — Site Manager (Passive Outreach)
Hi [Name], your [X years] in site ops management across [region] is exactly the background we're building our leadership team around. I'm Group HR at Valentis — we operate across SEA (Laos, Myanmar, Indonesia). Would value connecting for a conversation when the timing is right.
This is a connection request, not a pitch. Keep under 300 characters. Do not mention the role or salary — just open the door. Once they accept, follow up within 48 hours with a more detailed message.
📧 Email / Follow-up — Post Connection (Any Ops Role)
Subject: [Role] Opportunity — Valentis Laos · SEA Operations Hi [Name], Thanks for connecting. I wanted to share a bit more context on why I reached out. We're Valentis — a drilling and mineral exploration company operating across Laos, Myanmar, Indonesia, and Thailand. We're building out our operational leadership team and [Site Manager / Drilling Supervisor / Senior Driller] is a role where I think your background could be a genuine fit. What I noticed in your profile: [specific observation — company, title, scale, geography]. What we're offering: [Role title] based in [Location]. [Headcount/scope]. Package range: [salary range]. [Rotation schedule if applicable]. I'm not asking you to apply — I'm asking if you'd be open to a 20-minute conversation to see if there's a fit worth exploring further. No obligation, no pressure. If now isn't the right time, I'd welcome the chance to stay in touch for future conversations. [Your name] Group HR Manager, Valentis Laos [Phone / Email]
This is the follow-up after they accept your connection. Be specific about what you observed — it shows you actually read their profile. The "I'm not asking you to apply" framing removes defensiveness and increases response rate from passive candidates significantly.
📱 WhatsApp / Facebook Message — Local SEA Ops / Field Roles
Hello [Name], I'm the HR Manager at Valentis — we run drilling operations in [Myanmar/Laos/Indonesia]. We're looking for a [Drilling Supervisor / Senior Driller] for our [location] site. Package: USD [range]/month + [rotation / site allowance]. Start: [month]. Requirements: [2–3 key requirements only]. If you or someone you know might be interested, please contact me at [number] or send your CV to [email]. Thank you.
For Facebook groups and WhatsApp — keep it plain, direct, short. Local field workers respond better to simple language and a phone number than corporate-style messages. Always include a WhatsApp number, not just email. Post in local language (Burmese, Bahasa) where possible — use Google Translate and have a local contact review before posting.
🤝 Referral Request — To Existing Candidates or Network Contacts
Hi [Name], As we progress with your candidacy for the [Site Manager] role, I wanted to ask — do you know anyone in your network who might be a good fit for our Drilling Supervisor role in Myanmar? We're looking for someone with [8+ years drilling ops, supervisor experience, comfortable with remote SEA posting]. Package is $1,800–$3,000/mo + hardship premium. If you can connect us with someone suitable, I'd be grateful. The drilling community in SEA is small and referrals from people who know the work are the best signal we have. Thanks, [Your name]
Ask during the active interview process — candidates are most engaged and want to help. Arsen Tulebayev (Site Manager candidate) is your best referral source for the Myanmar Supervisor role. A referral from him carries instant credibility with his drilling network.
Personalization is not optional — it's the difference between 10% and 35% response rates. At minimum, reference one specific thing from their profile. Batch outreach with zero personalization is spam and damages Valentis's employer brand in a tight-knit drilling community where everyone knows everyone.
