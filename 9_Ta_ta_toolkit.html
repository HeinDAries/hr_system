STAR Method — Required for All Answers
Every competency question must follow STAR format
S
Situation
Context and background — when, where, what was happening?
T
Task
Their specific responsibility — what were they accountable for?
A
Action
What did THEY do — not the team. Push for individual ownership.
R
Result
Measurable outcome — numbers, timeframes, business impact.
⚑
Interviewer Ground Rules
Non-negotiable for every interview
✓ Ask the same core questions to every candidate for the same role
✓ Score DURING the interview — do not rely on memory after
✓ Never share your score before the other interviewer finishes independently
✓ Use probe questions when answers are vague — push for specifics
✗ Do not ask about age, marital status, religion, or family plans
✗ Do not make decisions on gut feel alone — score must support decision
✗ Do not discuss salary in Round 1 — HR handles this after shortlisting
▸
Universal Opening & Closing Questions
Use for every role — adapt wording to context
Opening Questions
"What specifically attracted you to this role — not a generic answer, what about this opportunity?"
"Tell me about a career decision that didn't work out and what you learned."
"What does working in a remote SEA site mean to you practically — what's your honest experience with that?"
Closing Questions
"Is there anything about your experience you haven't had the chance to share that's relevant?"
"What's your notice period and are there any constraints on your start date?"
"On a scale of 1–10, how interested are you right now — and what would make it a 10?"
For senior roles (Site Manager, Drilling Supervisor), add a second round with a technical case or scenario. For all roles, reference checks are mandatory before any offer is made.
When to use panel vs solo · and role split guidance
Solo Interview
1 interviewer · HR or Hiring Manager
Best for:
Initial screening calls. Support and admin roles. Low-risk hires. Round 1 pre-screen before committing panel time.
Risks:
Single perspective. Higher bias risk. Not defensible for senior hires alone.
Round 1 screening only
2-Person Panel
HR + Hiring Manager
Best for:
Most standard hires. Ops roles (Driller, Supervisor). Balances HR and technical lens. Manageable to coordinate.
Risks:
Groupthink if both agree before interviewing. Need clear role split or one person dominates.
Recommended for most roles
3-Person Panel
HR + Hiring Mgr + COO/CEO
Best for:
Senior-only (Site Manager, Country HR). Roles reporting direct to C-suite. Where culture fit is a deciding factor.
Risks:
Can intimidate candidate. Scheduling complex. CEO influence can override structured scoring.
Senior roles · Round 2 only
◉
Role Split in a 2-Person Panel
Clear ownership prevents duplication and gaps
HR Interviewer Owns:
• Opening, structure, and closing
• Behavioural / competency questions
• Culture and values fit assessment
• Red flag monitoring (conduct, stability)
• Logistics: notice period, availability, location
• Communication of next steps
Hiring Manager Owns:
• Technical competency questions
• Role-specific scenarios and problems
• Equipment or process knowledge depth
• Team dynamics and site environment fit
• Operational expectations and deliverables
• Technical red flags (credibility of claims)
Never brief the Hiring Manager on your impressions before they score independently. Discuss and compare ONLY after both have completed individual scorecards. This is the single most important anti-bias practice in structured interviewing.

⚡
Anti-Bias Reminders
Before scoring debrief
Halo Effect: Don't let one impressive answer inflate all scores. Score each competency independently.
Similarity Bias: Don't score higher because someone reminds you of yourself or a past top performer.
Recency Bias: Don't let the final answer dominate your overall impression. All answers are equal weight.
Seniority Deference: In panel settings, score independently before the COO/CEO shares their view.
Green flags · strong positive signals
✓ Quantifies everything
Gives numbers naturally — headcount, budget, timelines. Doesn't wait to be asked. Specific data signals genuine ownership of outcomes.
✓ Owns failures openly
Describes genuine failures without deflecting blame. Articulates what they changed. Self-awareness is a stronger predictor of performance than perfect track records.
✓ Asks sharp questions
Questions reveal thinking quality. Strong candidates ask about scope, team structure, and success metrics — not just package and holidays in Round 1.
✓ Researched Valentis
Knows the company, operating countries, and has specific observations. Shows genuine interest, not desperation for any job.
✓ Pushes back respectfully
Disagrees with framing or corrects a factual error politely. Confidence and integrity — critical for operational leadership roles in remote SEA environments.
✓ Proactive in process
On time. Acknowledged invite. Followed up. Behaviour during recruitment is a preview of behaviour on the job.
Red flags · disqualifying and concerning signals
🚩 Vague on headcount claims
Claims large workforce experience but can't name the org structure, direct reports, or specific challenges. "Around 500 people" with no detail is a credibility flag.
Action: "Who were your direct reports? Describe the org chart." Push until specific or expose the gap.
🚩 Always "we," never "I"
Uses "we" for every achievement, "I" only for failures. Individual contribution is invisible. May be overstating personal role in team outcomes.
Action: "What specifically did YOU do — not the team?"
🚩 Badmouths past employers
Describes previous employers or managers very negatively without self-reflection. This is a preview of how they'll speak about Valentis when they leave.
Action: "What did you take from that experience?" — see if any self-awareness emerges.
🚩 Safety culture dismissal
Describes safety requirements as "box-ticking" or hints productivity sometimes comes before safety procedures. Automatic disqualifier for Site Manager and HSE roles.
Action: Do not proceed for any ops role. Document reasoning clearly.
🚩 Evasive on departures
Inconsistencies in why they left roles. "Mutual agreement" that sounds like termination. Stories that don't align on dates or sequence.
Action: "Walk me through exactly how that transition happened." Reference check is critical here.
🚩 Inflexible on location reality
For site-based roles — shows real hesitation about remote conditions but hasn't disclosed it clearly. Will accept the offer and leave within 90 days.
Action: "This is genuinely remote with no city access. Describe your last remote posting." Do not proceed if hesitation remains.
Red flags are indicators, not automatic disqualifiers (except safety culture dismissal for ops roles). One flag with strong answers is different from three flags with weak answers. Document all observations — they are legally relevant if a hire fails probation.

Site Manager · question bank · Laos operations
Site Operations Leadership
STAR
Tell me about the largest workforce you have personally managed. What was your org structure and what were the biggest people challenges?
Look for: Specific headcount (need 300+). Clear structure described. Concrete people challenges. Evidence of proactive — not reactive — management. Quantified outcomes.
PROBE
Who were your direct reports specifically and how did you cascade accountability below you?
Look for: Can name people and structure. Understands delegation vs micromanagement. Can explain accountability frameworks.
HSE Leadership
STAR
Describe a serious safety incident or near-miss on your site. Walk through your immediate response, investigation, and what systemic changes resulted.
Look for: Structured investigation methodology. Takes accountability — doesn't blame crew. Systemic fix, not individual reprimand. Most important: what changed permanently after?
HYPO
You find your most experienced, productive driller operating without required PPE — not the first time. What do you do?
Look for: Zero tolerance framing. Acts immediately. Does NOT make an exception for productivity. Understands this is a culture signal to the whole site.
Decision Making Under Pressure
STAR
Tell me about a time operations on your site were at serious risk — equipment failure, supply crisis, or personnel emergency. How did you manage it?
Look for: Prioritises correctly (people safety first, ops continuity second). Makes decisions with incomplete information. Communicates up while managing the situation.
Stakeholder & Government Relations
STAR
Describe your experience managing government authorities or regulatory bodies at site level. What was the most challenging interaction?
Look for: Direct experience — not "my team handled it." Understands SEA government relationship dynamics. Diplomatic but firm. Does not describe avoidance.
PROBE
What do you know about operating in Laos specifically — regulatory environment, labour practices, cultural norms?
Look for: Genuine research or experience. If no Laos experience, acknowledges the gap and has a plan. Red flag: "It's similar to everywhere I've worked."
Budget & Cost Control
STAR
Tell me about the largest operational budget you personally managed. How did you track spend, identify variances, and report to leadership?
Look for: Owns budget accountability — not just "had visibility." Specific dollar amounts. Variance analysis process. Proactively escalated budget issues before they became problems.
Site Manager is Tier 1 critical. Use all 8 questions across two rounds. Round 1 (HR): competency + culture. Round 2 (HR + COO): technical depth, budget, government relations. Reference checks mandatory before offer.
Drilling Supervisor · question bank · Myanmar (overdue)
Drilling Operations Expertise
STAR
Walk me through the most technically complex drilling program you have supervised. What were the geological challenges and how did you manage them?
Look for: Specific technical knowledge — parameters, formation challenges, deviation control. Understands the gap between a written program and execution reality.
HYPO
You're 200m into a hole and suddenly lose circulation — mud not returning. You can't reach the Geologist. What do you do in the next 30 minutes?
Look for: Stops drilling immediately. Documents depth and parameters. Follows emergency protocol. Knows when to pull out vs attempt remediation. Communicates up immediately — doesn't sit on the problem.
HSE on the Rig Floor
STAR
A crew member refuses to follow a safety instruction during active drilling. What was the situation and how did you handle it?
Look for: Acts immediately — not "after shift." Consistent application. Does not use physical intimidation (red flag). Documents the incident.
Crew Leadership
STAR
Serious performance problem with a crew member on a remote site — what steps did you take?
Look for: Follows process — verbal, documented, escalation. Understands isolation challenges of remote site discipline. Involves HR/Site Manager at the right point.
PROBE
How do you manage a mixed crew of local nationals and expats where language and cultural differences create friction?
Look for: Specific strategies — visual SOPs, buddy systems, daily briefings, translators. Has done this. Understands cultural hierarchy dynamics in SEA drilling crews.
Equipment & Downtime
STAR
Major equipment breakdown during an active drilling program. How did you minimise downtime and what was the business impact?
Look for: Quantifies downtime and recovery time. Clear escalation path. Understands cost of unplanned downtime. Strong candidates describe what they changed in maintenance practice afterward.

Senior Driller · question bank · Indonesia / Myanmar
Rig Operation
STAR
Which rig types have you operated and what are the key operational differences? Which do you prefer and why?
Look for: Specific models and manufacturers. Can articulate technical differences. Preference backed by operational rationale, not just habit.
HYPO
Drill string vibrating abnormally, penetration rate dropped. Supervisor is off-site. What do you do?
Look for: Stops drilling. Checks parameters. Diagnoses possible cause methodically. Does not just push through. Contacts Supervisor. Documents findings.
Crew & Mentoring
STAR
How have you developed a junior driller or helper into a competent operator?
Look for: Show-don't-tell method. Patient but holds standards. Genuine investment in others. This is what separates a Senior Driller from someone who only does their own job.
Safety Ownership
STAR
Tell me about a time you identified a safety hazard on the rig before an incident occurred. What did you do?
Look for: Proactively identifies — not just responds. Reports it formally. Follows up to confirm it was fixed. Best candidates describe a non-obvious hazard.
Senior Driller can be assessed in a single 45-minute round with HR + Drilling Supervisor or Ops Manager. Weight 60% of time on technical competency. Verify certifications and check reference from most recent Drilling Supervisor before offer.
HSE Officer · question bank · Laos
HSE Management System
STAR
Tell me about a site where you built or significantly improved the HSE management system. What was the baseline, what did you do, what changed?
Look for: Systematic approach — risk register, permit to work, training regime. Quantifies improvement (incident rate, near-miss frequency). Understands the difference between a paper system and a live one.
PROBE
If the local regulatory standard is below your company standard — which do you apply?
Look for: Always applies the higher standard. Can articulate why. Does not take the easy path to avoid friction with local authorities.
Incident Investigation
STAR
Walk me through an incident investigation you personally led. What methodology, and what was the most important finding?
Look for: Named methodology (5-Why, ICAM, Bowtie). Root cause is rarely "operator error." Findings led to systemic change, not just retraining one person.
Safety Culture Influence
HYPO
New site — near-miss reporting is near zero because workers fear punishment for reporting. What's your first 30 days approach?
Look for: Does NOT mandate reporting from day one. Builds trust first. Separates reporting from blame. Works with Site Manager on visible leadership commitment. Understands this is cultural, not procedural.
STAR
Describe a time you challenged a senior manager on a safety decision. How did you handle it?
Look for: Does it — does not back down on safety. Uses data and standards, not emotion. Escalates if necessary. Red flag: has never had to do this. Safety compliance depends on this willingness.
Must verify NEBOSH or equivalent certificate before interview — not after. This role has functional authority over all site personnel on safety matters. Assess confidence and assertiveness as strongly as technical knowledge.
HRBP / HR Manager · question bank · Thailand
HR Generalist Depth
STAR
Manage me through a complex ER case — disciplinary, grievance, or termination — from start to resolution. Walk through your process.
Look for: Follows process rigorously. Documents every step. Consults labour law. Reaches a defensible conclusion. Does not cut corners to resolve quickly.
Thai Labour Law
HYPO
A manager wants to terminate a 3-year employee for "poor performance" — but there are no documented reviews. What do you advise?
Look for: Flags legal risk immediately (Thailand LPA severance exposure). Does not just comply. Advises on PIP and documentation path. Strong candidates cite specific LPA provisions.
Solo HR Function
STAR
Describe a time you were the only HR person for a large or complex operation. What did you prioritise, what did you let go, how did you manage your time?
Look for: Can triage effectively. Knows what has legal or operational urgency vs nice-to-have. Communicates workload limitations upward. Critical for Valentis context — this is the reality they'll walk into.
HYPO
In your first 90 days, you find payroll compliance gaps in two countries AND have three urgent hires to fill. You can't do both simultaneously. How do you decide?
Look for: Compliance first — legal exposure outweighs recruitment delay. Communicates the triage to leadership. Seeks temporary support for the deprioritised workstream.
Stakeholder Management
STAR
A senior manager pushed back hard on your HR recommendation. How did you handle it?
Look for: Holds position when backed by policy or law. Adjusts when the manager has a valid point. Does not simply capitulate. Red flag: "I always deferred to the business."
Assess self-direction heavily — this person needs to run Thailand HR with minimal supervision while you manage SEA operations. Prior solo HR function experience is the single most important qualifier for this role.
Support & Admin roles · universal question bank
Organisation & Reliability
STAR
Tell me about a time you were managing multiple urgent tasks simultaneously and something fell through the cracks. What happened and what did you do?
Look for: Owns the mistake. Does not blame volume alone. Describes a specific recovery action. Most importantly — describes the system or habit they changed afterward.
Remote Environment Fit
HYPO
This role is on a remote drill site — limited internet, no city amenities, mostly male workforce. What's your honest reaction to that and what would be your biggest adjustment?
Look for: Has actually thought about it. Honest about the adjustment. Red flag: "That's no problem" with zero reflection. Green flag: specific coping mechanisms and practical questions about facilities.
Discretion & Communication
STAR
Tell me about a time you handled sensitive or confidential information. Was there ever a moment where you were pressured to share it?
Look for: Takes confidentiality as a principle, not just a rule. Was not swayed by social pressure. Critical for EA roles — discretion failure directly affects C-suite operations.
STAR
Describe the most complex logistics or coordination challenge you've managed. How did you track everything and what happened when something went wrong mid-process?
Look for: Uses systems — checklists, trackers — not memory alone. Proactively communicates when issues arise. Does not wait to be asked for a status update.
Support roles can be assessed in a single 35-minute interview with HR only. Use a practical task — ask an Admin candidate to demonstrate a filing system or tracker setup. Behaviour in practical tasks is more predictive than verbal answers alone.

The 3 Core Rules
Applied to every role, every time
Source before you need
For every critical role, maintain 2–3 warm candidates at all times — even when there's no vacancy. The Myanmar Supervisor crisis exists precisely because sourcing only started when the vacancy opened.
The 3-channel minimum
For any active role, always run at least 3 sourcing channels simultaneously. LinkedIn + referral + specialist channel is the floor. Never rely on one job post alone.
Referral is the best channel
In SEA drilling, the best candidates don't apply to job ads. They are referred. Activate your network FIRST — especially for Supervisor and Manager roles.

Operations roles · Site Manager · Drilling Supervisor · Driller · Geologist
⚑
Why Ops Roles Are Hard in SEA
Thin local pool. Myanmar and Laos have very limited qualified drilling professionals at Supervisor level. Most qualified candidates are Central Asian (KZ, UZ) or Australian expats.
They don't job-hunt. Experienced drilling supervisors are almost always employed. They move via referral, direct approach from colleagues, or headhunter contact — not job boards.
Myanmar premium. Political environment makes Myanmar less attractive. Expect 15–25% premium over comparable roles in stable markets and higher rejection rates at offer stage.

When to Use an Agency
Use immediately when:Role is overdue with no pipeline. Ops-critical role that cannot stay vacant. You have zero bandwidth for active sourcing.
Consider using when:Thin local talent pool (all ops roles in MM/LA). Confidential search. Requires specific technical credentialing you can't verify independently.
Don't use agency for:Support or admin roles. Roles with warm bench candidates. Roles where the JD is unclear — fix the JD first or you waste everyone's time.
