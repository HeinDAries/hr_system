What Is an IDP?
An Individual Development Plan is a written agreement between an employee and their manager on where the employee wants to grow and how the company will support that. It is not a wish list — it is a commitment with actions, timelines, and accountability on both sides.
✓
An IDP is
A 12-month plan with 2–4 specific development goals, clear actions, and agreed support. Reviewed at mid-year and updated at annual review.
✗
An IDP is not
A performance improvement plan. Not a promotion promise. Not a list of training courses. Not a document that gets signed and filed and forgotten.
Who Gets an IDP?
Mandatory for Rating 4 and 5 employees. Strongly recommended for all Rating 3 employees with 1+ years of service. Optional for employees in their first 6 months.

Development Goal Types — What Can Go in an IDP?
Development goals fall into three categories. A strong IDP typically has one from each, or two from the most relevant category for that employee.
Skill Building
Technical & Functional
Certifications, technical training, equipment qualifications, software skills. Something the employee can demonstrably do after that they could not before.
Examples: Drilling cert, NEBOSH, Excel, Site First Aid
Exposure
Stretch Experience
Acting roles, cross-country project involvement, shadowing a more senior colleague, leading a piece of work they have not led before.
Examples: Act-up as Site Manager, lead HSE audit, join planning meeting
Leadership
People & Influence
Mentoring a junior, running team briefings, managing a small project, handling a stakeholder relationship independently for the first time.
Examples: Mentor a trainee, lead safety committee, manage vendor

Tool 6 — Conversation Guides

Development
Conversations

Development conversations are different from performance reviews. The question is not "how did you do?" but "where are you going, and how do we get you there?" Here is how to run them well.

The IDP Creation Conversation — 45 Minutes
Run this conversation immediately after the annual review, or as a separate meeting in January. This is the conversation where the IDP is built together.
The Mid-Year IDP Check-In — 20 Minutes
A focused conversation on development progress only. Separate from the mid-year performance review. Can be done back-to-back but not combined.
OPEN (3 min)
Pull out the IDP document. Acknowledge any progress since January. "Let us check in on your development plan — it has been about 6 months."
GOAL REVIEW (12 min)
Go through each development goal: What progress has been made? What got in the way? Does the goal still make sense? Update the status — on track, behind, or complete.
SUPPORT CHECK (5 min)
"Is the company holding up its side of the agreement? Is there anything that was committed that has not happened?" This is the accountability question for the manager.
Questions That Open Development Conversations
Aspiration Questions
  • Where do you want to be in three years?
  • What kind of work do you find most energising?
  • If you could take on one new responsibility this year, what would it be?
  • What does career success look like to you?
Gap & Support Questions
  • What do you think is the biggest gap between where you are and where you want to be?
  • What has helped you develop most in your career so far?
  • What is one thing I could do differently to help you grow faster?
  • What would make you feel more supported here?
