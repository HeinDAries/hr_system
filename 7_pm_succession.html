What Succession Planning Is Not
Clearing up common misconceptions before they derail the process.
✗
It is not about finding a cloneSuccessors will do the job differently. That is fine. You are identifying capability and potential, not a copy.
✗
It is not a secret promotion listSuccession is a planning tool for the company. Telling someone they are a successor creates expectations you may not be able to fulfil. Discuss development, not succession status.
✗
It is not only for the most senior rolesIn a 28-person company, losing one experienced driller creates a critical operational gap. Succession planning applies to all hard-to-fill roles, not just management.
The BaKuu Holding Succession Framework
Four questions that drive the succession process at BaKuu Holding.
1
Which roles are critical?If the person in this role left tomorrow, how long before operations are materially impacted?
2
Who could fill each critical role?Internal first. Then assess readiness — now, 12 months, or 2+ years with development.
3
What development accelerates readiness?Each successor should have a linked IDP (Tool 6) with goals targeting their specific gaps.
4
What is the external contingency plan?If no internal successor exists, what is the recruitment plan and how long will it take?

Tool 7 — Role Risk Assessment

Role Criticality

Not all vacancies are equal. A role is critical when its vacancy has immediate operational, safety, or financial impact — or when it is extremely hard to fill from the external market. Both conditions elevate criticality.

Criticality Matrix — BaKuu Holding Roles
Assessed on two factors: (1) operational impact of vacancy and (2) difficulty of external replacement. Roles scoring high on both are Critical.
Critical
Site Manager (Laos, Indonesia, Myanmar)
Direct operations control, safety accountability, crew management. Extremely scarce in SEA market. Operational shutdown risk within days of vacancy.
⏱ Replacement: 90–180 days
Critical
Drilling Supervisor (All countries)
Controls daily drilling output. Without a supervisor, driller crew cannot legally operate under many country regulations. High regional demand market.
⏱ Replacement: 60–120 days
High
Group HR Manager
Multi-country payroll, work permits, compliance. A vacancy exposes company to regulatory risk across 4 jurisdictions. Payroll accuracy impacts all employees.
⏱ Replacement: 60–90 days
High
Finance Manager (Indonesia)
Indonesian statutory reporting, tax compliance, and payroll. Local knowledge essential. Loss creates audit and compliance risk.
⏱ Replacement: 45–75 days
High
HSE Officer (Site Locations)
Regulatory requirement in several jurisdictions. Drilling cannot operate without qualified HSE presence on site. Relatively scarce in remote SEA market.
⏱ Replacement: 45–60 days
Medium
Senior Driller (All countries)
Output impact if Senior Driller vacancy unplanned. However, Driller can cover temporarily with Supervisor oversight. Moderately hard to fill.
⏱ Replacement: 45–75 days
Medium
Country Finance Officer
Local payroll and reporting risk. Finance Manager can absorb temporarily. Moderate market availability.
⏱ Replacement: 30–45 days
Standard
Admin / Support Roles
Operational disruption is manageable. Other team members can absorb short-term. Broader talent market availability.
⏱ Replacement: 20–40 days
Vacancy Cost Reminder (Oil & Gas Context)For drilling roles: every day of unplanned rig downtime costs significantly in lost meterage, crew costs, and contract penalties. For a 28-person company operating 4 country sites, a single critical role vacancy can threaten a whole exploration programme. This is why succession planning for these roles is not optional.

Tool 7 — Scenario Planning

Departure
Scenarios

What happens if key people leave tomorrow? This section documents the contingency plan for each critical departure scenario — so the response is planned, not improvised.

Why document departure scenarios?In a 28-person company, losing one or two key people simultaneously is plausible — and the impact is magnified. A planned response takes 2 hours to prepare. An unplanned response takes weeks and costs significantly more. The scenarios below should be reviewed and updated each year.
